# Sengoku v25
2-6 players | 60-120 minutes | 12+ years

Sengoku is a territory-control and card-placement game about building a clan domain, defending key positions, and timing your expansion carefully. Players place Numbered cards and Retainers, manage Koku and Protection separately, and race to form a connected Capital that triggers the end of the game.

## COMPONENTS

1. 1 Game Board.
2. 1 Player Board per player.
3. 1 Clan Deck per player, with 20 cards each: 1 Daimyo, 12 Numbered cards, 6 Retainer cards, and 1 Unique Special Retainer.
4. Koku tokens, 9 per player in the base setup.
5. Protection tokens, 6 per player in the base setup.
6. 1 Emperor Visit marker per player for Expert Mode.
7. 1 Market piece per player.
8. 3 Castle pieces per player.
9. 1 Capital piece per player.

Important component parts used throughout the rules:

- Game Board: zones, lines, Imperial Zones, and starting spaces.
- Player Board: Koku reserve, resource reminder, Expert Mode references, and clan-specific panels such as Alliance and Destiny Path.
- Clan Deck: Daimyo card, Numbered cards, Retainer cards, and the Unique Special Retainer.
- Daimyo card: the clan anchor card and Chain of Command starting point.
- Numbered cards: the cards used for placement, control, and building destinations.
- Retainer cards: tactical cards with two Order Actions.
- Koku tokens: currency.
- Protection tokens: map resources used for defense and building progression.
- Emperor Visit marker: Expert Mode usage marker.
- Market piece: economic building.
- Castle piece: defensive progression building.
- Capital piece: endgame structure and Shogun trigger.

## SETUP

1. Choose the correct board for the game mode and place the **Game Board** in the center of the table.
   - 2 players use the 5x5 Duels board.
   - 3-4 players use the 6x6 Standard board.
   - 5-6 players use the 9x9 Grand Campaign board.

2. Give each player a **Player Board**, one **Clan Deck**, the clan's **Daimyo** card, **Koku tokens**, **Protection tokens**, one **Emperor Visit marker**, one **Market** piece, three **Castle** pieces, and one **Capital** piece.
   - If Expert Mode is not being used, the Emperor Visit markers still belong to the players but are not active.
   - Unused expansion clans and any spare pieces return to the game box.

3. Remove each **Daimyo** card from its deck and keep it face up beside the matching Player Board.
   - The Daimyo is parked beside the Player Board during setup and is not yet on the board.
   - Shuffle the remaining cards in the deck.
   - Draw 3 cards.
   - If the 3-card hand contains no Numbered card, reshuffle that hand into the deck and redraw 3, repeating until the hand contains at least one Numbered card.

4. Place the starting **Protection** on each Daimyo card.
   - Default setup uses +P1 on each Daimyo.
   - Any declared scenario or variant that changes this must be announced before the game begins.

5. Determine the first player, then sit the players in turn order clockwise.

6. Perform the Junbi placement phase.
   - Starting with the first player, each player places their Daimyo on a legal starting space for the chosen board.
   - On the 6x6 board, the listed starting locations are B1, D1, C1, E1, A2, F2, A3, F3, A4, F4, A5, F5, B6, C6, D6, and E6.
   - Corner spaces are not valid starting locations on the 6x6 board.
   - The 5x5 and 9x9 boards use their own setup sheets for starting positions.

7. Return any unused setup components to the game box and begin play.

## GOAL

Win by having the highest final score after the game ends.

## GAMEPLAY

### 1) Core concepts

**Sudoku validity**

Face-up Numbered cards must obey the Sudoku rule: no duplicate Numbered card in the same row, column, or zone.

If you create an illegal state, that state is an Error, also called a Rebellion.

**Chain of Command**

Your Chain of Command is the orthogonally connected group of your cards that includes your Daimyo.

- "Chain" means Chain of Command.
- "Near your chain" means orthogonally adjacent to your Chain of Command.
- "Adjacent" means one space away orthogonally or diagonally.
- Only connected cards and structures score unless a rule says otherwise.
- Many actions and building requirements depend on being connected to your Chain of Command.

**Control**

A zone, line, or Imperial Zone is controlled by your clan if the relevant spaces are fully occupied by your clan's cards.

- In a stack, only the top card counts for control.
- A face-down top card still counts as your clan for control, but it does not count as a revealed Numbered card.
- Face-down cards do not score as cards.

**Imperial Zones**

Imperial Zones are the four marked central spaces with the Emperor symbol.

- A controlled Imperial Zone gives +1 Koku during Collect Resources, but only if that Imperial card is connected to your Chain of Command.
- Imperial Zones cannot receive Protection tokens.
- Imperial Zones cannot host Markets, Castles, or Capitals.
- Face-down cards cannot be placed in Imperial Zones.
- Normal Numbered-on-Numbered overlap is not allowed in Imperial Zones.
- Explicit Retainer cover or overlap effects may still apply there if the Retainer text allows it.

### 2) Turn structure

A turn has four sections: Beginning of Turn, Active Turn, Collect Resources, and End of Turn.

#### Beginning of Turn

1. Refill your hand up to 3 cards.
2. If Expert Mode is enabled and your Emperor Visit marker is still unused, you may declare Visit the Emperor after the refill and before any other action.
3. If deck exhaustion happens during a required draw and the Emperor Visit right is unused, resolve the automatic exhaustion reshuffle first, and then the Emperor Visit right is lost if it was unused.

#### Active Turn

During your Active Turn, you may interleave legal actions, but each one must resolve fully before you begin the next one.

The Active Turn includes:

- Play cards.
- Pay 1 Koku to play one additional last card, ending with 1 card in hand.
- Convert Koku into Protection at a rate of 2 Koku to 1 Protection.
- Place gained Protection immediately if legal.
- Build Markets, Castles, and Capitals if the requirements are met.
- Reveal face-down cards by paying 3 Koku.
- Fix Errors on the board.
- Make or break Alliances in Expert Mode.

You cannot proceed to Collect Resources until you satisfy the hand-size condition:

- You must be at 2 cards in hand, or
- at 1 card in hand if you paid the extra-card Koku cost.

If no legal placement is possible for a card you are trying to place, you discard one card and continue the turn with any other legal non-placement actions.

#### Collect Resources

Collect Koku from the player board and the board state.

- Base income is +1 Koku.
- A built Market gives +1 Koku.
- Each controlled Zone gives +1 Koku.
- Each controlled Line gives +1 Koku.
- Each controlled Imperial Zone gives +1 Koku, only if that Imperial card is connected to your Chain of Command.

The end-of-turn counting step can generate at most 5 Koku.

If the counting would take you above the Koku storage cap, discard the excess so that you keep at most 5 Koku in storage.

#### End of Turn

1. Resolve any effects that explicitly trigger at end of turn.
2. Refill your hand up to 3 cards.
3. Pass the turn clockwise.

### 3) Cards and placement

#### Daimyo

The Daimyo card is removed from the deck during setup, placed beside the Player Board, and then placed on the Game Board during Junbi.

- It starts the Chain of Command.
- It cannot be played face-down.
- It can hold up to 3 reserve Protection tokens.
- Koku is never placed on the Daimyo card.

#### Numbered cards

Numbered cards represent prefectures and are used for control, stacking, and building destinations.

- They may be placed anywhere legal.
- They do not need to be connected to your Chain of Command unless a specific rule or card says otherwise.
- They may be stacked only on your own face-up Numbered cards, and only if the resulting placement is legal.
- A Numbered card can never be placed on top of a Retainer.
- A Numbered card cannot be placed on a face-down card.
- Face-down Numbered cards are allowed as hidden placeholders.
- While hidden, a face-down Numbered card has no active number and does not create Sudoku conflicts.

#### Retainer cards

Retainers have two Order Actions, called A1 and A2.

- When you use a Retainer, choose and resolve exactly one of its two actions.
- Retainer Order Actions are separate from Turn Procedures such as Convert Koku, Reveal, Build, and Error Fix.
- Some Retainers are placed on the board.
- Some Retainers are discarded to resolve their effect.
- Retainers may coexist with other Retainers in the same row, column, or zone unless their text says otherwise.
- Retainer overlap or cover is allowed only when the printed Retainer text allows it.

If a Retainer is returned to your hand later, it can be used again, and you may choose either Order Action again.

#### Stacks and overlap

- Only the top card in a stack is active for control and legality checks unless a rule says otherwise.
- If the top card is removed, the revealed lower card becomes active immediately.
- Revealing a lower card can immediately create a new Error.
- There is no hard limit on pile height.
- A card cannot be placed on top of a face-down card unless an effect explicitly says otherwise.

### 4) Face-down cards, Reveal, and traps

Only Numbered cards may be placed face-down as hidden placeholders. Some Retainers, such as Shinobi and Ronin, may also be placed face-down as hidden traps when their printed text allows it.

Rules for face-down cards:

- A face-down Numbered card is a hidden placeholder.
- A hidden Numbered card does not create Sudoku duplicates until it is revealed.
- A face-down card still occupies the space and still counts as your clan for control if it is on top.
- Face-down cards cannot receive Protection tokens.
- Face-down cards cannot be placed in Imperial Zones.
- No player, including the owner, may look at a face-down card without paying the Reveal cost unless a card effect explicitly allows it.
- If any other card is accidentally placed face-down, flip it face-up immediately at no cost and continue play.

Reveal:

- Pay 3 Koku to reveal any legal face-down card.
- You cannot reveal your own face-down card in the same turn in which you placed it.
- Reveal can be used on your own face-down card only on a later turn.
- If the revealed card is a normal card, flip it face-up and apply its identity immediately.
- If the revealed Numbered card creates a forbidden duplicate, an Error is created immediately.
- If the revealed card is a trap, resolve its trap effect once and then remove the card from the map and discard it.
- If a trap allows it, the trap effect targets the revealer first.

### 5) Errors and Rebellions

An Error is an illegal board state, usually a forbidden Numbered duplicate in the same row, column, or zone.

There are two different Error windows.

#### Immediate correction window

If an illegal placement is detected during the acting player's turn before Collect Resources, any player may call it immediately.

The acting player must correct the placement at once by either:

- placing that card face-down in a legal face-down space of their choice, or
- discarding that card if no legal face-down correction exists.

This immediate correction gives no reward.

The immediate correction window ends once Collect Resources begins.

#### Rewarded Error fixing on your own turn

On your own turn, you may fix any existing Errors.

1. Declare the Error.
2. Remove one offending card.
3. Gain 1 Protection token for each Error that removal resolves.
4. Place each gained token immediately if legal.
5. If the placed token triggers building progression, resolve that progression immediately before handling the next unresolved token.
6. If another Error remains, continue or stop as you choose.

A single removal may resolve more than one Error at once. You gain 1 Protection token per Error resolved.

If no legal face-up placement exists for a gained Protection token, that token is discarded to supply.

A player cannot intentionally create an Error and then profit from fixing it in the same turn.

Removed offending cards also remove any attached Protection, Market, Castle, or other building on that layer.

### 6) Koku, Protection, and reserves

**Koku**

- Koku is currency.
- Koku is stored on the Player Board area, not on map cards.
- Koku pays for Reveal, extra-card play, and Koku-to-Protection conversion.
- Koku storage cap is 5.

**Protection tokens**

- Protection tokens are map resources.
- They are placed on eligible face-up cards.
- A card can have at most one piece on it: either a Protection token, a Market, a Castle, or a Capital.
- A protected top active state cannot normally be removed or replaced unless a rule or card explicitly says otherwise.
- In a stack, Protection applies to the top visible layer.
- Protection tokens can be converted into Markets and Castles through building progression.

**Daimyo reserve**

- The Daimyo card may hold up to 3 reserve Protection tokens.
- These are Protection tokens, not Koku.
- Daimyo reserve tokens do not protect the Daimyo automatically.
- Reserve tokens may be committed to building conversions when the building rules allow it.

**Player supply**

- Each player has a personal off-map supply of components.
- If an effect steals or uses more tokens than are on the map, the remaining amount comes from the player's supply when the effect allows that.

**Quick spend reference**

- Reveal: 3 Koku.
- Convert Koku to Protection: 2 Koku to 1 Protection.
- Building progression uses Protection, not Koku.

### 7) Defences

Defence is built through layered control: Protection tokens, Castles, Capitals, and interrupt Retainer effects.

- Protection is the first defensive layer on a card.
- A Castle is a stronger defensive structure and a step toward Capital.
- A Capital is the highest structural defense and the Shogun trigger condition.
- Interrupt Retainers can react to specific declared actions if their text opens that window.

If an effect removes the top layer that contains a building, the building is removed with that layer.

### 8) Building Markets and Castles

Buildings are created through Protection-token progression and are not purchased directly with Koku.

**Market**

- Cost: 2 Protection.
- Place the Market on an eligible Numbered card in your Chain of Command.
- A Market can be built only on a Numbered card with no existing building on that card.
- A player can have at most 1 Market.
- A Market is never mandatory.

**Castle**

- Cost: 3 Protection.
- Place the Castle on an eligible Numbered card in your Chain of Command.
- A Castle can be built only on a Numbered card with no existing building on that card.
- A player can have at most 3 Castles.

**Mandatory Castle conversion**

Castle conversion is mandatory only if you have 3 Protection tokens on connected Numbered cards in your Chain of Command and a valid connected Numbered destination exists.

- If the committed tokens include one or more tokens from Retainers and/or Daimyo reserve, the conversion is legal but not mandatory.
- Mandatory Castle conversion resolves immediately when its condition is met.
- If a mandatory Castle conversion cannot resolve legally, the game state must be corrected immediately and the tokens remain in place.

**4th Castle handling**

If a Castle conversion would create a 4th Castle, you may relocate one of your existing Castles to a legal connected Numbered destination.

- If no legal relocation exists, the Castle conversion cannot resolve and the 3 tokens remain in place.

**Building restrictions**

- Imperial Zones cannot host Markets or Castles.
- Buildings always stay on eligible Numbered cards.
- A Market follows the same placement constraints as a Castle.

### 9) Forming a Capital

Requirements:

- 3 Castles in your Chain of Command.
- Continuous connectivity to your Daimyo.

Process:

1. The moment the third connected Castle is completed, the Capital conversion is mandatory and immediate.
2. Remove the 3 Castles immediately.
3. Choose a Numbered card space in your Chain of Command as the Capital base.
4. If the base card is face-up, turn it face-down first.
5. If a face-down card already exists there, use it directly as the base.
6. Place the Capital on top.

The Capital is always placed on a face-down base card.

- While the Capital remains on top, the base card cannot be attacked, revealed, or destroyed by effects.
- A connected Capital immediately triggers Imperial Summons.
- If one of the required Castles becomes disconnected, Capital conversion cannot occur until connectivity is restored.
- A player cannot delay Capital conversion once 3 connected Castles are valid.
- The current ruleset uses a single active Capital path per player for Shogun progression.

### 10) Attacks and Retainer action timing

Attack effects are resolved one at a time, in play order.

- There is no "declare all attacks, then resolve" step.
- Each attack effect must be resolved fully before the next one begins.
- Retainers can affect Numbered cards and, where their text says so, other card types.
- A card with a current Protection layer on its top active state is not a legal target for remove/replace-style effects unless a rule or card explicitly says otherwise.
- A card with a Protection token, Market, Castle, or Capital on top is therefore not a legal target for an effect such as Ronin replacement unless that effect explicitly bypasses protection.
- On your turn, you may voluntarily remove your own Castle or Capital and return the piece to supply if you need to reposition or recover the chain.

### 11) Shogun and Imperial Summons

When you legally form a valid connected Capital, you immediately trigger Imperial Summons and become the first Shogun for scoring.

- Becoming Shogun is mandatory when the Capital is formed.
- You finish that current turn normally and in full.
- After that turn, every player gets one final restricted turn in normal turn order, starting with the next player after the first Shogun and ending with the first Shogun.
- All active Alliances end immediately when Imperial Summons begins, and no break reward is gained from that automatic ending.

Restricted final turns:

- Players cannot play or place cards.
- Players may only spend Koku to Reveal face-down cards, or convert Koku into Protection and use those tokens for legal build progression, including Castles and Capital if requirements are met.
- The normal hand-size gate is suspended during restricted final turns.
- Restricted final turns do not perform a normal end-of-turn refill.
- Building a Capital during a restricted final turn does not create a new Shogun turn and does not restart Imperial Summons.
- If another player legally forms a connected Capital during the same Imperial Summons, that player also counts as a later Shogun for scoring.
- Shogun scoring is ranked by order of achievement during the same Imperial Summons: first +4, second +3, third +2, all later +1.

After all restricted final turns are completed, the game ends immediately and final scoring begins.

### 12) Alliances

Alliances are part of Expert Mode.

- Alliances are formed according to the Expert Mode text printed on the Player Boards.
- The alliance marker is a Protection token of the allied player's color placed on the Daimyo card.
- While used as an alliance marker, it cannot be spent or moved as a normal Protection token unless an effect explicitly removes it.
- Alliance state is public information.
- Allied players may not Reveal each other's face-down cards unless a rule or card explicitly allows it.
- Retainer actions should not affect allies unless the card text explicitly says otherwise.

Breaking alliances:

- If an Alliance token is removed by an effect, the alliance ends immediately.
- If a player breaks an alliance voluntarily during their active turn, the normal Player Board break reward applies.
- If the alliance ends automatically because a Capital is formed or Imperial Summons starts, no break reward is gained.
- Breaking an alliance does not automatically award bonus resources unless an effect explicitly says so.
- In 5-6 player Expert Mode, each player may maintain at most 2 simultaneous alliances.

### 13) Visit the Emperor

Visit the Emperor is an Expert Mode rule.

Proactive use:

- You may declare Visit the Emperor only at the beginning of your own turn, after the beginning refill and before any other action.
- If your Emperor Visit marker is already used, you cannot use it again.
- Shuffle your deck, discard pile, and entire hand together.
- Then draw 3 cards.
- If you had more than 3 cards in hand, shuffle all of them in and still draw only 3.
- After proactive use, flip or turn the Emperor Visit marker to used state.

Automatic deck exhaustion handling:

- At the first required-draw exhaustion, if the Emperor Visit right is unused, perform the normal reshuffle from discard to a new deck.
- In that automatic exhaustion case, the Emperor Visit right is lost immediately.
- If exhaustion happens during the beginning refill, that automatic procedure happens first, and you cannot declare proactive Visit the Emperor later in that same turn.
- If deck exhaustion happens again, no second reshuffle is available.

### 14) Final scoring

Score only connected elements.

- +1 for each face-up Numbered card on the map.
- +1 for each face-up Retainer card on the map.
- +1 for each fully controlled Zone.
- +1 for each fully controlled Line.
- +1 for each Castle.
- +3 for each Capital.
- Shogun scoring is based on the order of achievement during Imperial Summons: first +4, second +3, third +2, all later +1.
- +1 in Expert Mode if you never used your Emperor reshuffle right.
- Losing the Emperor right through automatic exhaustion handling counts as used, so it gives no +1 bonus.

Scoring rules:

- Face-down cards do not score the direct +1 card value.
- Face-down cards still count for zone and line control if they belong to your clan.
- Disconnected cards, Castles, and Capitals do not score until they are reconnected.
- Alliance tokens do not add score by default.

Tie-break:

- If final score is tied, the player with the highest remaining Koku on the Player Board wins.
- If still tied, the tied players share the victory.

## GAME END

The game ends immediately when Imperial Summons has finished and all restricted final turns have been completed.

Imperial Summons begins the moment a player legally forms a connected Capital.

What happens then:

1. The player who formed the Capital becomes the first Shogun immediately.
2. That player finishes the current turn normally and in full.
3. Every player then takes one final restricted turn in normal turn order.
4. During those restricted turns, no one may play or place cards.
5. During those restricted turns, players may only use Koku to Reveal, or convert Koku into Protection and use that Protection for legal build progression.
6. After the restricted turns end, final scoring is checked.

Important endgame details:

- The Capital trigger is checked when the Capital is formed, not at the start of a later turn.
- The Shogun trigger is mandatory and cannot be skipped.
- If a player forms another valid connected Capital during Imperial Summons, that player also becomes a later Shogun for scoring.
- Building a Capital during a restricted final turn does not restart the endgame or create a new Imperial Summons.
- Alliances end the instant Imperial Summons begins, and that automatic ending gives no break reward.
- Shogun scoring is ordered by the sequence of Capital formation during that same Imperial Summons.
- Final score is then compared, with Koku as the tie-breaker and a shared victory if the tie remains.

## VARIANTS, LISTS, GLOSSARY

### Standard Mode

Standard Mode uses the base rules of placement, control, defence, building, Shogun trigger, and final scoring.

### Expert Mode

Expert Mode adds:

- Alliances.
- Visit the Emperor.
- Destiny Path objectives from the Player Board.

Expert Mode also uses the alliance marker rule, the Emperor Visit marker rule, and the Expert scoring bonus for never using the Emperor reshuffle right.

### Duel Mode

Duel Mode is the 2-player version of the game.

- Use the 5x5 Duel board.
- Each player chooses a clan and sets up normally.
- The opening placement includes the Daimyo and one connected Numbered card for each player.
- Disconnected cards remain on the board, but they do not score or provide connected benefits until reconnected.
- Disconnected buildings do not provide income or scoring while disconnected.
- The center bridge zone gives +1 income when controlled and connected.
- Buildings cannot be placed on the bridge space.
- Expert-only modules are optional in Duels and should be enabled only if both players agree before setup.

Duels endgame:

- Duel endgame uses the same connected Capital trigger.
- After the first Shogun turn, the opponent takes one restricted final turn, then the first Shogun takes one restricted final turn.
- The same restricted-turn limits apply.
- If the opponent forms a connected Capital during their restricted turn, they become the second Shogun for scoring.
- Tie-break uses remaining Koku, then shared victory.

Duels scoring:

- +1 or +2 for Numbered or Retainer cards on marked scoring spaces, as printed on the Duel board.
- +1 for each complete controlled zone or line that is connected to the chain.
- +1 for controlling the central bridge zone.
- +1 for each Castle.
- +3 for each Capital.
- Shogun scoring and Expert Mode bonuses follow the global rules.

### Retainers reference

Retainers have two actions each. Use only the action you choose, and follow the printed text if it is more specific than the summary below.

#### Samurai

- A1: Place near your chain or cover your own clan card, then kill 1 unprotected adjacent card, or remove 1 Token or 1 Market.
- A2: During an opponent's turn, discard to cancel a declared Retainer card action before it resolves. Discard that card too.
- Samurai cannot block Trap effects.

#### Ashigaru

- A1: Place near your chain or cover any own-clan unprotected Numbered card, then kill up to 2 adjacent unprotected Numbered or face-down cards.
- A2: Draw 4 cards, keep 1, and return the others together to the top or bottom of the deck.

#### Chajin

- A1: Draw 2 cards. You can use this action at most once per turn.
- A2: Draw 1 card. If it is a Retainer and you play it right away, its bonus becomes 3.

#### Sohei

- A1: Place on a Sohei, an unprotected Numbered card, or a face-down card of any clan.
- A2: Retrieve the top 1 card from your discard pile into your hand.

#### Shinobi

- A1: Remove from the map 1 Retainer and any Token on it, or 1 face-down card.
- A2: Spy on a player's hand, choose 2 cards to discard, then draw +1 card at the end of the turn.
- If Shinobi is revealed as a trap, it affects the revealer first whenever the printed effect allows it.

#### Ronin

- A1: Place on your own or any clan's unprotected top non-Ronin card, replacing it. Return that card, then discard or return the rest of the stack.
- A2: Steal up to 2 Tokens from cards of other clans, max 1 per clan, with the rest taken from supply if needed.
- If Ronin is revealed as a trap, it affects the revealer first whenever the printed effect allows it.

#### Unique Special Retainers

Each clan also has a Unique Special Retainer or another clan-specific variant. Read those cards directly from their printed text.

### Glossary

- Adjacent: a target space or card at distance 1, orthogonally or diagonally.
- Alliance Token: a Protection token of the allied player's color placed on a Daimyo card.
- Chain / Chain of Command: your orthogonally connected group of cards starting from and including your Daimyo.
- Clan: the faction of a player and their cards.
- Face-down card: a hidden card placed face-down.
- Imperial Summons: the endgame sequence that starts when a connected Capital is formed.
- Koku: currency stored on the Player Board.
- Market: a building created by spending 2 Protection.
- Near your chain: orthogonally adjacent to your Chain of Command.
- Numbered card: a prefecture/province card identified by a number.
- Place: put a card on a board location.
- Player Board: the personal board used to track resources and reference rules.
- Protection token: a map resource used for defence and building progression.
- Reveal: turn a face-down card face-up by paying the Reveal cost or through an effect.
- Retainer card: a tactical card with two actions.
- Token: generic shorthand for a Protection token unless a rule says otherwise.
- Trap: a hidden Retainer effect that resolves when revealed.
- Unprotected / Protected: a card without or with a current protection layer on its top active state.
- Zone: a group of board spaces used for control and income.
- Line: a full row or column from one side of the board to the opposite side.
