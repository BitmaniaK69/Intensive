## 1) GAME OVERVIEW

Set during Japan's Sengoku period (1467-1615), the game places each player in command of a Daimyo clan competing for influence, territorial control, and political supremacy.

[HISTORICAL NOTE] The campaign focus points toward Sekigahara (1600), the decisive battle that determined the end of the Sengoku power struggle.
Daimyo were regional lords governing domains across Japan. Domain size varied widely (small, medium, and large), and the map's province/prefecture spaces are a gameplay abstraction rather than modern prefectural borders.

Players shape the map by placing Numbered and Retainer cards, creating tactical positions, and timing actions carefully. Koku is the game currency, while Protection tokens are map resources used for defense and building progression.

The game is played in two modes:

- Standard Mode: core rules focused on placement, control, defense, and scoring.
- Expert Mode: adds Alliances, Visit the Emperor, and Destiny Path objectives.

Core arc:

- Build influence by placing Numbered and Retainer cards on the board.
- Expand control by respecting Sudoku-like placement constraints.
- Use Protection tokens to defend key cards and convert progress into buildings.
- Push toward Capital formation and Shogun declaration.

Victory statement:

- The winner is the player with the highest final score.

RULE:

- If two or more players tie on score, the player with the highest remaining Koku on the Player Board wins.
- If still tied, tied players share the victory.

## 2) GAME COMPONENTS

The base game is designed for 4 players. Expanded setups (5-6 players) require the larger board and the additional clan components.

Listed components (base, 4 players):

- 1 main game board.
- 4 player boards.
- 4 player decks (20 cards each: 12 Numbered, 6 Retainer cards, 1 Extra Special Retainer, 1 Daimyo).
- 36 Koku tokens (9 per player).
- 24 player Protection Tokens (6 per player).
- 4 Emperor Visit markers (1 per player, used in Expert Mode).
- 4 Markets (1 per player).
- 12 Castles (3 per player).
- 4 Capitals (1 per player).

Player-color reference (base + expansions):

- Oda Nobunaga: dark red.
- Toyotomi Hideyoshi: green.
- Akechi Mitsuhide: blue.
- Tokugawa Ieyasu: yellow.

Expansion set:

- Date Masamune: violet.
- Mori Terumoto: light grey.
- Ishida Mitsunari: black.
- Lady Oichi: orange.

RULE:

- Protection tokens are map resources for defense/build progression.
- Koku is represented by physical Koku tokens, kept on the player board area and never placed on the map.
- Emperor Visit markers start standing on the player board.

RULE:

- Piece limits are hard limits (for example: max 3 Castles per player, max 1 active Capital in the current ruleset).

## 3) SETUP

Setup remains clan/deck based, with the Daimyo card separated before the first draw.

Before play:

1. Each player chooses a Daimyo/clan color.
2. Each player takes their board, deck, and components.
3. Place each Emperor Visit marker standing in its available space (Expert Mode tracking).
4. Place starting Protection on the Daimyo card:
   - default setup: `+P1` on each Daimyo.
   - optional scenario/start-position variant: selected seats may start with `+P2` if declared before Turn 1.

Deck preparation:

1. Remove the Daimyo card from the deck and keep it face up beside the Player Board (it will be placed on the Game Board during the initial phase called Junbi).
   - The Daimyo is parked beside/on the Player Board during setup only; it is not yet on the Game Board.
2. Shuffle the remaining 19 cards and place them face down on the side of the Player Board.
3. Draw an initial hand of 3 cards.
4. If the initial hand contains no Numbered cards, reshuffle that hand into the deck and redraw 3 cards (repeat until at least one Numbered card is in hand).

RULE:

- This setup redraw procedure is part of setup and is not Visit the Emperor (it does not consume/flip the Emperor marker).


Turn order:

- Determine only the first player.
- Play then proceeds clockwise.
- "Order Number" on player boards is guidance/theme and not a mandatory seating rule.
- On the 6x6 board, only the listed starting locations are valid; corner prefectures are not valid starting positions.

## 4) GAME ELEMENTS

Board and zone control drive both position and income.

### Game Board

Board formats:

- 2 players: 5x5 Duels board, with dedicated starting coordinates and zone geometry defined on the Duel setup sheet.
- 3-4 players: 6x6 board.
- 5-6 players: 9x9 Grand Campaign board, with dedicated starting coordinates, Imperial Zones, and zone geometry defined on the Grand Campaign setup sheet.

Zones:

- On the 6x6 board, Standard setup uses 6 zones (Z1-Z6), each as a 3x2 area.
- Zone geometry for 5x5 (The Duel) and 9x9 (The Grand Campaign) is defined on their respective setup sheets.
- Zones are used for control checks and income.
- Imperial Zones are central marked spaces; each controlled Imperial Zone grants +1 Koku during Collect Resources, only if that Imperial card is connected to your Chain of Command.
- There are four Imperial Zones in the center, marked with the Emperor icon; they cannot receive face-down cards, protection tokens, any building, or normal Numbered-on-Numbered overlap.

Line control:

- A valid line runs from one side of the board to the opposite side.
- A line is controlled only if all its spaces are occupied by your clan (top cards; face-down top cards still count as their clan).
- Vertical and horizontal lines are valid.
- Diagonals are not valid lines.

Control and stacking:

- In stacked spaces, only the top card counts for control checks; if it is face-down, it still counts as its clan but has no revealed number.
- A card with a Number on it placed on a space on the board represents a Prefecture occupied by the Clan 
- A Retainer card placed on a space on the board occupies and rules the Prefecture.
- A prefecture can contribute to both its zone and a valid line simultaneously.
- When both apply, both income contributions are counted.

### Player Board

Each player has a personal board for managing information and resources during play.

It shows:

- Daimyo history and quick references.
- Turn-order number reference.
- Koku/resource tracking spaces.
- Expert-mode references (when enabled).
- Clan identity and symbols.

RULE:

- The Player Board repeats the economy values for quick reference.
- This rulebook defines the full economy timing, caps, and edge cases.

Player Board income schema:

- End of turn: +1 Koku (base income).
- With Market built: +1 Koku.
- Each Zone you control: +1 Koku.
- Each controlled Imperial Zone: +1 Koku, only if that Imperial card is connected to your Chain of Command.
- End-of-turn counting cap: max 5 Koku gained from this counting step.
- Storage cap after counting: keep up to 5 Koku in storage, discard excess.

## 5) THE DECK AND CARD TYPES

Each Daimyo deck has 20 cards:

- 1 Daimyo card
- 12 Numbered cards (1-6, two copies each)
- 6 Retainer cards
- 1 Unique Special Retainer

Deck consistency:

- Numbered cards are the same across clans.
- Clan identity is shown by color/symbol styling.
- Numbered cards and Retainer cards share the same clan back style, so hidden cards are not role-revealed from the back.
- Printed card text is normalized from project variables in `data.json`, so labels such as `special`, `numbered`, `covered`, and `protected` should be read as the rendered game terms.
- In this project, `special` means `Retainer` in the printed card text.

### Daimyo Card

- Placed on the Game Board during the initial phase (called **Junbi**) and remains on the board.
- Starts the Chain of Command.
- Can hold up to 3 reserved tokens for later building use.
- Is counted for end-game card scoring.

### Numbered Cards

- Represent prefectures/provinces.
- Used for control, stacking, and building destinations (when eligible).
- A Numbered card may be placed in any legal space on the board; it does not need to be connected to your Chain of Command unless a specific rule says otherwise.
- A face-down Numbered placeholder follows the same board placement rule, but while hidden it has no active number.

### Retainer Cards

- Provide tactical effects through Action 1 / Action 2 choices.
- A Retainer is used in one of two ways:
  - Place / overlap / cover it on the board when the card text allows placement.
  - Discard it to resolve a tactical effect when the card text uses sacrifice/discard logic.

RULE:

- Unique Special Retainer follows its printed icon logic; place and discard modes are not interchangeable unless explicitly stated.

## 6) RETAINER CARDS ANATOMY

Retainer cards provide tactical actions and can be played either by placing them or by resolving their effect according to icon/text.

General structure:

- Each Retainer has 2 Order Actions, but only one can be chosen when the card is used.
- The use mode depends on what is reported on the text (Place/Stack or Sacrifice/Discard logic).

Action and usage rules:

- When you play a Retainer, choose and resolve exactly 1 of its 2 Order Actions.
- If that same Retainer card later returns to your hand, it may be played again and you may choose either Order Action again.
- Retainer effects are single-use unless card text explicitly states an exception.
- Some cards explicitly restrict repeated use in the same turn (for example specific Chajin text).
- A player may stop at 2 cards in hand; to play one final card (down to 1), they must pay 1 Koku.
- Reveal, Convert Koku, Build, and Error Fix are Turn Procedures (not Retainer Order Actions) and may all be performed in the same turn if legal.

Face-down cards (Numbered placeholders + hidden traps):

- Only **Numbered** cards may be placed face-down as hidden placeholders.
- Some **Retainer** cards such as Shinobi and Ronin may be placed face-down as hidden traps.
- A face-down **Numbered** card is a hidden placeholder with no active number while it remains face-down.
- While face-down, a Numbered placeholder does not create Numbered Sudoku conflicts (row/column/zone duplicates).
- While face-down on top of a space/stack, it still occupies the space and counts as its clan for control checks.
- Face-down cards cannot be placed in **Imperial Zones**.
- Face-down traps are not identifiable until a player pays the Reveal cost.
- No player (including the owner) may look at a face-down card without paying the Reveal cost, unless a card effect explicitly allows it.
- A player cannot Reveal a face-down card they placed during the same turn.
- If you want to Reveal your own face-down card, you must wait until a later turn.
- When a face-down card is revealed, flip it face-up and apply its normal identity immediately.
- If a revealed Numbered card now creates a forbidden duplicate, an Error is created immediately.
- When a face-down Shinobi or Ronin is revealed, resolve its trap effect once, targeting the revealer first whenever the trap text allows it, then remove the card from the map and discard it.
- If any other card is accidentally placed face-down, flip it face-up immediately (no cost) and continue play.
- Face-down cards cannot receive Protection tokens.
- A Capital is always placed on a face-down base card (already face-down, or turned face-down during placement).
- While the Capital remains on top, the base card below cannot be attacked, revealed, or destroyed by effects.

Token-steal clarification:

- Token stealing targets tokens currently on the board (including tokens on Daimyo cards if present there).
- If a card effect says "1 from each clan" and the board has fewer valid targets, missing tokens are taken from that player's personal supply.
- Stolen Protection tokens are immediately placed/used as allowed by the stealing effect and are never converted into Koku.
- Trap token-steal effects target the revealer first whenever the printed effect allows it.

NOTE:

- Keep a complete Retainer reference appendix aligned with the current card set to avoid text ambiguity during play.
RULE:
- The Retainer appendix is included in the final printable rulebook and placed at the end.

## 7) CARD PLACEMENT RULES

When placing from hand, follow both card text and Sudoku constraints.

RULES:

- Daimyo cards cannot share row, column, or zone with other Daimyos.
- Numbered cards cannot duplicate within the same row, column, or zone.
- Face-down Numbered placeholders have no active number while hidden, so they do not create Numbered duplicates until revealed.
- Retainer cards may coexist with other Retainer cards in the same row/column/zone unless card text says otherwise.
- Numbered cards and face-down Numbered placeholders may be placed anywhere on the board if the space is otherwise legal; they do not need to be chain-connected.

If no valid placement is possible:

- Discard one card.
- Continue the turn with any other legal non-placement actions.

Stacking:

- A Numbered card may be stacked only on your own face-up Numbered card, if the resulting placement is legal.
- A Numbered card can never be placed on top of a Retainer card.
- A card cannot be placed on top of a face-down card unless a card effect explicitly says otherwise.
- Retainer overlap/cover is allowed only when the printed Retainer text allows it.
- In a stack, only the top card is active for control/sudoku checks; if the top card is face-down, only clan ownership applies until reveal.
- If the top card is removed, the revealed lower card becomes active immediately.
- Revealed lower cards can immediately create errors that must be resolved.
- There is no hard limit on pile height.
- In Imperial Zones, normal Numbered-on-Numbered overlap is not allowed; only explicit Retainer cover/overlap effects may apply there.

TIP:

- Use stacking to preserve chain presence when open placement is blocked.

## 8) ERRORS - REBELLIONS

[HISTORICAL NOTE] The Sengoku period (“Warring States”) was defined by constant conflict and rapid power shifts. The idea of *gekokujō* (“the low overcomes the high”) captured how underlings could overthrow superiors in this chaos.

Errors are illegal board states (usually Numbered duplicates in a forbidden row/column/zone) and can be exploited tactically if not corrected in time.

Immediate detection:

- If an illegal placement is detected during the acting player's turn, any player may call it before Collect Resources.
- In this immediate timing window, the acting player must correct the placement at once.
- The usual correction is to place that card face-down in a legal space chosen by the acting player.
- If no legal face-down correction is available anywhere, discard the card.
- This immediate correction does not grant a correction reward.
- Illegal trap placement follows the same correction flow; there is no separate `-1 Castle` or `-1 card` penalty unless a card or scenario explicitly says so.

Rebellion correction:

- On your turn, you may declare and fix one or more already existing board errors.
- The fixer chooses removal order and which offending card(s) to remove.
- If one correction reveals another error, the same fixer may continue correcting in the same turn.
- A single removal may resolve multiple Errors at once; gain 1 Protection token for each Error resolved.

Resolution effects:

- Removed offending cards lose attached protection/buildings.
- The fixer gains 1 Protection token for each corrected error.
- Each gained Protection token must be placed immediately if legal.
- If no legal placement exists, that token is discarded.
- Protection gained this way follows the immediate-placement rule before the next unresolved token is handled.

Treason/coordination:

- Intentional error play can be used strategically.
- Allied players may coordinate around error timing.
- However, all players can still call an error immediately before turn handoff.

RULE:

- There is no extra direct penalty for intentional illegal placement beyond correction removal and reward transfer.
- A player cannot intentionally create an Error and then profit from fixing it in the same turn.

TIP:

- Error fixing is both defensive cleanup and offensive removal timing.

## 9) CARDS AND TOKENS

Token semantics are strict: Koku is currency, while tokens placed on the board are Protection tokens.

Rules:

- A token placed on a board card protects that card.
- Protected cards cannot be removed directly unless an effect removes protection first or explicitly bypasses protection.
- In stacks, protection applies to the top visible layer.
- Stacking and covering follow the Card Placement Rules and the printed Retainer text.
- Protection tokens cannot be placed on face-down cards by action, effect, or correction.
- A Capital must be placed over a face-down base card and becomes the protection for that stack.
- Protection tokens can be placed only on Numbered cards or Retainer cards that are not already protected (maximum one piece per card: 1 Protection token, or 1 Market, or 1 Castle).
- Daimyo can hold up to 3 parked reserve Protection tokens; these tokens are not Koku and do not make Daimyo "protected."
- Unless a rule or effect explicitly says otherwise, gained Protection must still follow the immediate placement/discard rule above and does not default to the Daimyo reserve.

### Daimyo Card

- Daimyo is placed on the Game Board during the initial phase, Junbi, and then remains on the map.
- can hold up to 3 reserved build tokens (Protection tokens only) in its reserve slots; these do not provide automatic protection.
- Reserved tokens can be removed by card effects (for example steal/kill effects) if targeted legally.
- Koku tokens are never placed on the Daimyo card.

Gameplay terms used in this chapter:

- Adjacent: a target space/card at distance 1 (horizontal, vertical, or diagonal).
- Chain / Chain of Command: your orthogonally linked group of cards starting from and including your Daimyo.
- Near your chain: a placement space orthogonally adjacent to your Chain of Command.

RULE:

- During rebellion/error correction, when an offending protected card is removed, the card and its attached protection/building are removed together.

## 10) TOKENS TYPES

[HISTORICAL NOTE] *Koku* (石) was a real unit tied to rice yield (about 180 liters). It was widely used to measure wealth and a domain’s productivity.
A lord was typically considered daimyō-class at 10,000 koku or more.

The current system separates currency and map protection.

### Koku

- Koku is the main currency and is represented by physical Koku tokens.
- Koku tokens are kept on the player board area (not on map cards).
- Koku is spent to pay specific actions (for example Reveal) and can be converted into map protection.
- Counting cap and storage cap are separate:
  - End-of-turn economy counting can generate at most 5 Koku from counting sources.
  - Stored Koku cap is 5. Any Koku above 5 is discarded.

### Protection Tokens

- Protection tokens are placed on map cards.
- They provide defense and are also used as building progress for Castles and Market.
- Koku is the main currency. Protection tokens are generated by converting Koku during your turn.
- Turn Procedure — Convert Koku: You may convert any amount of Koku into Protection tokens at a rate of 2 Koku -> 1 Protection token.
- Protection token pool is capped at 6 total tokens per player.
- When a Turn Procedure/effect grants Protection (for example Convert or Error Fix reward), those granted tokens must be placed immediately if legal.
- If that placement triggers building progression, resolve it immediately before handling the next unresolved token.
- If granted Protection cannot be placed immediately on a legal face-up card, it is discarded to supply.

### Daimyo Reserve (Protection-only reserve on the Daimyo card)

- The Daimyo card may hold up to 3 reserve Protection tokens.
- Tokens in the Daimyo reserve are still Protection tokens (not Koku).
- Daimyo reserve tokens do not protect the Daimyo automatically.
- Daimyo reserve tokens may be committed to building conversion when allowed by the Building rules.

### Player Supply (Off-map Components)

- Each player keeps a personal supply pile next to their board (Protection tokens, Koku, Castles, and other components) that are not currently on the map.
- Koku tokens should normally remain in the Player Board area; they are never placed on map cards or on the Daimyo card.
- When an effect steals Protection tokens and the target has fewer tokens on the map than required, the remaining tokens are taken from that player's personal supply.

### Alliance Tokens (Expert Mode)

- Alliance tokens mark active alliances and are placed on Daimyo cards.
- An Alliance token is a Protection token of the allied player's color.
- Alliance tokens are not spendable as normal Protection tokens.
- If an Alliance token is removed, the alliance ends immediately.

RULE:

- Breaking/removing an Alliance token does not grant bonus tokens to the attacker unless a card explicitly says so.

ILLUSTRATION: token-type overview with board examples and icon callouts.

### Quick Reference: Spend Koku vs Spend Protection

**Spend Koku (currency):**

- **Reveal (Bribery): 3 Koku** to reveal any legal face-down card. You may reveal your own face-down card, but not in the same turn you placed it.
- **Convert to Protection (Turn Procedure):** 2 Koku → 1 Protection token (you may convert any amount you currently have).

**Spend/Use Protection tokens (map resources):**

- **Place for defense:** place 1 Protection token on an eligible face-up card (max 1 piece per card: 1 token *or* 1 building).
- **Building progression:** remove **3 Protection tokens** to create **1 Castle**, or **2 Protection tokens** to create **1 Market** (per Building rules).
- Protection tokens are **not** currency and are **never** converted back into Koku.

## 11) DEFENCES

Defense is built through layered control: Protection tokens, Castles, Capital positioning, and interrupt Retainer effects.

Defensive layers:

- Protection token: first defensive layer on a card.
- Castle: strong defensive structure and progression step toward Capital.
- Capital: highest structural defense and Shogun trigger requirement.
- Interrupt Retainers (for example Samurai-like effects): reactive defense against key actions.

RULE:

- In stacked cards, building-based protection applies to the current top card.
- If an error removes the top layer containing a building, the building is removed with that layer.

TIP:

- Build defensive depth by combining Protection tokens with chain connectivity, not by relying on a single structure.

NOTE: 

- Imperial Zones cannot be protected with Protection tokens.

## 12) BUILDING CASTLES AND MARKET

[HISTORICAL NOTE] During the Sengoku era, castles became administrative and military hubs. Castle towns (*jōkamachi*) grew around them as concentrated centers of power, trade, and logistics.

Buildings are created through Protection-token progression and are not purchased directly with Koku.

Base requirement:

- Castle: 3 Protection tokens available on cards in your Chain (Chain / Chain of Command).
- Market: 2 Protection tokens available on cards in your Chain (Chain / Chain of Command).
- At least one valid Numbered card in your Chain (Chain / Chain of Command) available as destination.

Process:

- Remove the committed tokens.
- Place 1 Castle or 1 Market on an eligible **Numbered** card in your Chain of Command with **no existing building** (Castle/Market/Capital) on that card.
- The destination can be any eligible Numbered card in your Chain (Chain / Chain of Command); it does not need to be one of the cards that provided the tokens.

Mandatory transformation rule:

- Castle conversion is mandatory only if you have at least 3 Protection tokens on connected **Numbered** cards and a valid connected Numbered destination exists.
- Remove any 3 eligible committed tokens from your connected chain when that mandatory Castle conversion resolves.
- Market conversion uses 2 Protection tokens and is never mandatory.
- Committed tokens may come from Numbered cards, Retainer cards, and Daimyo parked reserve tokens, but the building destination must always be a valid connected Numbered card.
- If the committed tokens include one or more tokens from Retainer cards and/or Daimyo reserve, the conversion is legal but not mandatory.
- Mandatory building resolution happens immediately when the condition is met; if it cannot resolve legally, the game state must be corrected immediately.

Stack clarification:

- A Castle protects the top layer of a stack.
- If an error removes that top layer, the Castle is removed with it; lower layers remain and are checked normally.

Market clarification:

- Market follows the same placement constraints as Castle.
- Defensively, a Market behaves like one protection layer and can be removed by legal Retainer effects or by error correction.
- Market costs 2 Protection tokens to build.

NOTE:

- Max 3 Castles per player.
- Max 1 Market per player.
- If a Castle conversion would create another 4th Castle, you may relocate one of your existing Castles to a legal connected Numbered destination.
- If no legal relocation is possible, the Castle conversion cannot resolve and the tokens remain in place.
- If 3 connected Castles are present at any point, Capital conversion remains mandatory (Section 13).

NOTE: 

- Imperial Zones cannot be used to build any Castle or Market.

TIP:

- Use Market to stabilize income and Castles to stabilize board presence.

## 13) FORMING A CAPITAL

Capital formation is the final structural progression step.
Castles are progressed through Protection tokens and are not purchased directly with Koku.

Requirements:

- 3 Castles in your Chain of Command.
- Continuous connectivity to your Daimyo.

Process:

- Capital Placement is mandatory and immediate: when you complete the third connected Castle, remove the 3 Castles and place 1 Capital at once.
- The Capital is always placed on a face-down base card.
- If the chosen base card is a face-up Numbered card in your Chain (Chain / Chain of Command), turn it face-down first, then place the Capital.
- If there is already a face-down card at the chosen Capital position in your Chain (Chain / Chain of Command), including a hidden trap, use it directly as the Capital base.
- While the Capital remains on top, that base card is locked and cannot be attacked, revealed, or destroyed by effects.
- Because the 3 Castles came from 3 connected Numbered cards, there is always a legal Numbered destination for the Capital transition.

Connectivity rule:

- If one of the required Castles becomes disconnected, Capital conversion cannot occur until chain connectivity is restored.

Castle-count handling:

- In disconnected situations, a player may rebuild a connected Castle line before conversion.
- The player cannot delay conversion once 3 connected Castles are valid.

RULE: 

- The current ruleset uses a single active Capital path (max 1 active Capital state per player for Shogun progression).

TIP:

- Prioritize resilient chain routes before converting to Capital, to reduce declaration risk.

ILLUSTRATION: castle-to-capital conversion sequence with before/after map snippets.
RULE:

- Disconnected Castles do not contribute to Capital progression.
- To recover progression, the player may reconnect the chain and/or create a new connected Castle by relocating one existing Castle (see Section 12 Castle-limit handling).

## 14) ATTACK ACTIONS

Attacks are resolved through Retainer actions, error correction, and trap/reveal effects.

Attack scope:

- Valid effects can target cards, protection, and structures according to card text.
- Retainers can affect Numbered cards and, where specified, other card types.
- A card with any current protection layer on its top active state is not a legal target for remove/replace-style effects unless a rule or card explicitly says otherwise.
- A card with a Protection token, Market, Castle, or Capital on its top active state is therefore not a legal target for effects such as Ronin replacement unless the effect explicitly bypasses protection.

Resolution order:

- Resolve attack effects one at a time, in play order.
- Apply each effect fully before resolving the next effect.
- This applies to all card types, including Numbered cards and Retainers.

Sacrifice (voluntary):

- On your turn, you may remove your own Castle or Capital and return the piece to supply.
- Use this only when repositioning or chain recovery is strategically necessary.

ILLUSTRATION: attack-resolution sequence with step-by-step effect order.

RULE:

- There is no "declare all attacks then resolve" sub-step.
- Attack effects are always resolved sequentially, one at a time.
- Ronin replacement rule: the target must be the unprotected top non-Ronin card in that space. Ronin replaces that target and remains in play in the same space. "The rest of the stack" means every card below the targeted top card. If cards are returned, each card goes to its owner's hand.

## 15) BECAME A SHOGUN / IMPERIAL SUMMONS

[HISTORICAL NOTE] The title *Seii Taishōgun* was formally bestowed by the Emperor, providing imperial legitimacy to a military ruler (even when real power was contested).
Example: in 1603 Tokugawa Ieyasu received the shogun title from Emperor Go-Yōzei, often treated as the start of the Tokugawa shogunate.
Related episode: Akechi Mitsuhide is often remembered as the self-proclaimed "thirteen-day shogun" for his short-lived bid to seize power after Honnō-ji.

A player triggers the Imperial Summons when they legally form a valid connected Capital.

Shogun declaration (immediate trigger):

1. Form a Capital that is connected to your Chain of Command.
2. Immediately become Shogun. This trigger is mandatory and cannot be skipped.
3. Finish that current turn normally and in full.
4. After that turn ends, the Imperial Summons begins: every player takes one restricted final turn, including the first Shogun.

Restricted final turns:

- During a restricted final turn, players cannot play or place cards.
- They may only spend Koku to Reveal face-down cards, or convert Koku into Protection tokens and use those tokens for legal placement/build progression, including Castles and Capital if requirements are met.
- Restricted final turns are taken in normal turn order, starting with the next player after the first Shogun and ending with the first Shogun.
- Visit the Emperor is not available during restricted final turns.
- The normal Active Turn hand-size gate is suspended during restricted final turns. The restricted player does not need to play down to 2 cards, and does not pay the extra-card cost.
- A restricted final turn ends when that player passes after resolving any allowed Koku-based actions. Do not perform a normal End of Turn refill during restricted final turns.
- Building a Capital during a restricted final turn does not create a new Shogun turn and does not restart the Imperial Summons.
- However, if a player legally forms a connected Capital during a restricted final turn, that player also counts as Shogun for scoring.
- Shogun scoring is ranked by order of achievement during the same Imperial Summons: first = +4, second = +3, third = +2, all later = +1.

Endgame sequence:

- The round is called the Imperial Summons.
- The Imperial Summons starts immediately when a player legally forms a connected Capital.
- When the Imperial Summons starts, all active alliances end immediately and no break reward is gained.
- The player who triggered the Imperial Summons finishes their current turn in full and is the first Shogun for scoring in this game.
- Then every player takes one restricted final turn in normal turn order, starting with the next player after the first Shogun and ending with the first Shogun.
- After all restricted final turns are completed, the game ends immediately and final scoring begins.

Final action window:

- During restricted final turns, only the limited Koku-based actions above are allowed.
- Defensive/interruption Retainers are used only if a card/rule explicitly opens a legal reaction window.

NOTE:

- V25 uses sudden-death timing: the Shogun trigger is checked when the Capital is formed, not at the beginning of a later turn.

## 16) ALLIANCES

Alliances are part of Expert Mode (together with Visit the Emperor and Destiny Path objectives).

[HISTORICAL NOTE] Sengoku politics relied heavily on shifting coalitions. Alliances were often pragmatic and temporary, and could be reinforced through diplomacy (e.g., negotiated pledges and marriage ties). Many alliances ended in betrayal, including Akechi Mitsuhide's famous defection and multiple Daimyo reversals at Sekigahara.

Formation:

- Alliances are formed according to the Expert-mode conditions printed on player boards.
- When formed, alliance markers/tokens are placed on Daimyo cards as a pact indicator.

RULE:

- The alliance marker is a Protection token of the allied player's color placed on the Daimyo.
- While used as an alliance marker, it cannot be spent or moved as a normal Protection token unless an effect explicitly removes it.

Effects:

- Alliance effects and constraints are applied as defined by current Expert-mode board text.
- Alliance state is public information.
- Allied players may not Reveal each other's face-down cards unless a card or rule explicitly allows it.

Breaking alliances:

- If an Alliance token is removed by effect, the alliance ends immediately.
- If a player breaks an alliance voluntarily during their active turn, the normal player-board break reward applies.
- If the alliance ends automatically because a Capital is formed or the Imperial Summons starts, no break reward is gained.
- Breaking an alliance does not automatically award bonus resources unless an effect explicitly says so.

RULE:

- Alliance handling belongs to Expert Mode only; Standard Mode ignores this chapter.

NOTE:

- If this chapter conflicts with older text, Expert-mode player-board text has priority.
RULE:
- In 5-6 player Expert Mode (9x9 board), each player may maintain at most 2 simultaneous alliances.

## 17) VISIT THE EMPEROR

[HISTORICAL NOTE] The Imperial Court in Kyoto remained a symbolic source of legitimacy. Warlords often sought court titles or imperial recognition to strengthen their claims.
Example: Toyotomi Hideyoshi pursued court legitimacy and became *kampaku* (Imperial Regent) in 1585.

Visit the Emperor is an Expert Mode rule and is played together with Alliances and Destiny Path.

Core effect:

- The action enables a deck reset/reshuffle flow through each player's Emperor Visit right.
- The reshuffle can be used once per match.
- Emperor Visit marker state must be visible (available vs used/lost), for example by flipping/turning the marker.

Deck-exhaustion handling:

- At first deck exhaustion during a required draw (start/end turn or effect), if the Emperor Visit right is unused, perform a normal deck reshuffle (discard -> new deck).
- In this automatic exhaustion case, the Emperor Visit right is lost immediately and the marker is flipped/turned to used-lost state.
- If this happens during beginning refill, resolve this automatic procedure first; afterward, no proactive Visit the Emperor can be declared in that same turn.
- If deck exhaustion happens again, no second reshuffle is available.
- After the one allowed reshuffle, players keep drawing in normal order from whatever deck remains; if no cards are available, they do not draw.

RULE:

- Emperor-visit usage and marker state must be visible to all players.

NOTE:

- Treat this as Expert-only timing control and resource-management pressure, not as a base-mode action.
- Visit the Emperor can be declared proactively only at the beginning of your own turn, after the beginning refill and before any other action, as long as your Emperor marker has not already been used.
- In proactive Visit the Emperor, shuffle your deck, discard pile, and entire hand together, then draw 3 cards.
- If you have more than 3 cards in hand when you Visit the Emperor, shuffle all of them in and still draw only 3.
- After proactive use, flip/turn the Emperor marker to used state; it cannot be used again.
- Outside this timing window (mid-turn, end-turn, or while resolving effects), proactive Visit the Emperor is not allowed.

## 18) TURN STRUCTURE

A turn has four sections:

1) Beginning of Turn

- Refill back up to 3 cards. If you already have 3 cards, you draw 0.
- This beginning refill exists to recover from off-turn effects that may have reduced your hand before your turn starts.
- Shogun timing reminder: the Imperial Summons is triggered immediately when a valid connected Capital is formed; it is not a start-of-turn declaration.
- Expert Mode timing: if Emperor marker is unused, you may declare Visit the Emperor now, after refill and before any other action.

2) Active Turn

- Play cards down to 2 cards in hand.
- You may play one additional last card by paying 1 Koku, ending with 1 card in hand.
- Turn Procedure — Convert Koku (2 Koku -> 1 Protection token, any amount during your turn), then place Protection tokens.
- Build Market/Castle/Capital when conditions are met.
- Reveal (Bribery): Pay 3 Koku to reveal any legal face-down card. You may reveal your own face-down card, but not in the same turn you placed it.
- Fix existing errors, and make/break alliances in Expert Mode.
- All legal procedures in the active turn may be interwoven, but each one must be resolved fully before the next begins.
- You cannot proceed to Collect Resources until the hand condition is met: 2 cards in hand, or 1 card in hand after paying the extra-card cost.

3) Collect Resources

- Collect Koku/resources from the player board and board state.
- Apply the end-of-turn economy: base income, Market, controlled Zones, and controlled Imperial Zones.
- Imperial income requires Chain-of-Command connectivity.
- End-of-turn economy counting generates at most 5 Koku from counting sources.
- Income is collected from all valid sources; players do not selectively skip sources to avoid cap waste.
- Any Koku gained above the 5 Koku cap is discarded.

4) End of Turn

- Refill back up to 3 cards for your next turn.
- Pass turn clockwise.

RULE:

- Full error-fix actions and action execution happen only during the Active Turn.
- Retainer Order Actions and Turn Procedures are separate systems.
- Reveal, Convert Koku, Build, and Error Fix may all be performed in the same turn if legal.
- Off-turn players may still call an immediate illegal placement before Collect Resources, and the acting player corrects it immediately with no reward.
- After Collect Resources, no new immediate-error correction window remains for that turn.
- The Player Board repeats economy values as a quick reference; use this rulebook for timing, caps, and edge cases.
- If deck runs out, follow the active mode rule:
- Standard Mode: reshuffle discard as new draw deck.
- Expert Mode with Visit the Emperor: at first required-draw exhaustion, do the same normal reshuffle and mark Emperor Visit as lost if it was unused.
- In normal flow, the end-of-turn refill already restores your hand to 3; the beginning refill often draws 0 and mainly exists to recover from off-turn hand loss.

RULE:

## 19) END GAME SEQUENCE (STANDARD)

Endgame starts immediately when a player legally forms a valid connected Capital.

Shogun trigger:

- 1 connected Capital is sufficient.
- The Capital must be valid and connected at the moment it is formed.

Shogun turn:

- The first player who meets this condition immediately becomes Shogun and finishes that turn normally and in full.

Restricted final turns:

- After the Shogun turn ends, every player takes one final restricted turn in normal turn order, starting with the next player after the first Shogun and ending with the first Shogun.
- During a restricted final turn, players cannot play or place cards.
- They may only spend Koku to Reveal face-down cards, or convert Koku into Protection tokens and use those tokens for legal placement/build progression, including Castles and Capital if requirements are met.
- The normal Active Turn hand-size gate is suspended during restricted final turns. The restricted player does not need to play down to 2 cards, and does not pay the extra-card cost.
- A restricted final turn ends when that player passes after resolving any allowed Koku-based actions. Do not perform a normal End of Turn refill during restricted final turns.
- Building a Capital in a restricted final turn does not create a new Shogun turn and does not restart the Imperial Summons.
- A player who legally forms a connected Capital during a restricted final turn also counts as Shogun for scoring.
- Shogun scoring is ranked by order of achievement in the same Imperial Summons: first = +4, second = +3, third = +2, all later = +1.

Final-phase interaction:

- Samurai-style interruptions are allowed only if a card/rule explicitly opens that legal reaction window.

Closure:

- After all restricted final turns are completed, scoring begins immediately.

## 20) FINAL SCORING (STANDARD)

Scoring uses connected control relative to each player's Chain of Command.

Score:

- +1 each face-up Numbered or Retainer card on board.
- +1 each full zone or line under control.
- +1 each Castle.
- +3 each Capital.
- Shogun scoring is awarded by order of achievement during the Imperial Summons: first Shogun = +4, second = +3, third = +2, all later = +1.
- +1 if (Expert Mode) the player never used their Emperor reshuffle right.
- Losing Emperor right by automatic deck-exhaustion handling counts as "used" (no +1 bonus).

Control definition:

- "Under your control" means the relevant zone/line is fully occupied by your clan cards (face-down cards still count as your clan).
- In stacked spaces, only the top card determines control; a face-down top card still counts as its clan.
- Chain of Command adjacency is edge-to-edge only (no diagonal chain links).

RULE:

- Face-down cards do not score the direct +1 card value.
- Face-down cards still count for zone/line control when they belong to your clan.

Connectivity rule:

- Only connected cards/structures score.
- Disconnected cards, Castles, and Capitals do not score until reconnected.

Alliance token scoring:

- Alliance tokens do not add direct score by default.
- They matter only through alliance effects unless a specific card/effect states otherwise.


NOTES/TIPS:

- Becoming the Shogun does not guarantee victory.
- Large connected protected areas usually score better than scattered expansion.

## 21) LET'S START! PREPARATION PHASE - JUNBI

Junbi is the initial placement phase used to establish starting Daimyo positions.

[HISTORICAL NOTE] *Junbi* (準備) means "preparation." In Sengoku warfare, pre-battle preparation covered deployment order, supply readiness, and command-line positioning before engagement.

Preparation steps:

1. Determine first player.
2. First player places Daimyo on a legal starting edge location.
3. Next players place Daimyo clockwise, each respecting Daimyo Sudoku restrictions (no same row, column, or zone conflict with already placed Daimyo cards).
4. Once all Daimyo cards are placed, normal turn flow begins.

Setup clarification:

- During setup, each Daimyo card is parked beside/on the Player Board until its Junbi placement turn.
- This preparation phase uses Daimyo placement only.
- Numbered-card setup placement is not part of this phase in the current ruleset.

Starting locations:

- Starting Daimyo locations are specific edge positions marked for setup.
- Coordinate reference (6x6): B1, D1, C1, E1, A2, F2, A3, F3, A4, F4, A5, F5, B6, C6, D6, E6.
- Coordinates for 5x5 (Duels) and 9x9 (Grand Campaign) are documented in their dedicated setup sheets.
- Only the listed starting locations on the relevant setup sheet are valid; corner prefectures are not valid starting positions.

Fallback rule:

- If a player must redraw for setup-hand constraints, reshuffle and redraw until at least one valid Numbered card is available for normal play.

## 23) RULE CHANGES (DUELS)

Duels mode uses tighter connectivity and board-pressure rules.

Chain of Command:

- Cards do not need to be placed connected to the Daimyo chain unless a rule/card says so.
- Disconnected cards remain on the board, but they do not score or provide connected benefits until reconnected.
- Disconnected buildings do not provide income/scoring while disconnected.

Bridge (center space) rule:

- The center bridge zone grants +1 income when controlled and connected.
- Cards may be placed there if legal.
- Buildings cannot be placed on the bridge space.

Economy and actions:

- Core Koku/protection rules remain active.
- Expert-only modules (Alliances / Visit the Emperor) are optional and should be enabled only if both players agree before setup.

RULE:

- Duels follows the same attack-resolution order defined in Section 14.
- Disconnected cards lose chain-based effects immediately at disconnection.

## 24) END GAME (DUELS)

Duels endgame follows the same Shogun trigger principle used in standard mode.

Trigger:

- A player becomes the first Shogun for scoring when they legally form a valid connected Capital.
- That player then finishes the current turn normally and in full.

Closure:

- After the Shogun turn, the opponent takes one final restricted turn, then the first Shogun takes one final restricted turn.
- During each restricted turn, that player cannot play or place cards.
- They may only spend Koku to Reveal face-down cards, or convert Koku into Protection tokens and use those tokens for legal placement/build progression, including Castles and Capital if requirements are met.
- Building a Capital in a restricted turn does not create a new Shogun turn.
- If the opponent legally forms a connected Capital in their restricted turn, they become the second Shogun for scoring and gain the lower Shogun-value tier.
- After both final restricted turns are completed, the game ends and scoring begins.

## 26) GLOSSARY

Glossary terms should reflect current economy split and reveal cost in Koku (K3).

Extracted keywords:

- Adjacent: a target space or card at distance 1, orthogonally or diagonally.
- Bonus/Add to the effect: increase the printed strength or value of another card ability.
- Alliance Token: marks alliance on Daimyo card.
- Chain / Chain of Command: your orthogonally connected group of cards starting from and including your Daimyo.
- Clan: faction of Daimyo; all player cards belong to same clan.
- Near your chain: in a space orthogonally adjacent to your Chain / Chain of Command.
- Face-down Card: a hidden card placed face-down; while hidden it follows the normal face-down rules and is not treated as a revealed top active card.
- Daimyo: Japanese lord character represented by player.
- Discard/Eliminate/Kill: remove card from play to owner's discard pile.
- Emperor: non-playing sovereign enabling the Expert-mode reshuffle flow through its dedicated cost/marker rule.
- Error: same Numbered cards in same row/column/zone; must be fixed.
- Game Board: board where cards are placed.
- Interrupt: stop/nullify opponent card effect instantly.
- Koku: physical currency token stored on the player board to pay actions and conversions.
- Market: economic building that grants +K1 income during Collect Resources while controlled.
- Number/Numbered Card: number-identified prefecture/province card.
- Place: set a card on board location.
- Player Board: unique personal board with info and token spaces.
- Player Supply: each player's off-map component pool next to the Player Board.
- Protection Token: token on board card used for defense/buildings.
- Prefecture: single board space.
- Return: send the target card back to the relevant owner's hand unless a rule/card explicitly says otherwise.
- Reveal: turn a face-down card face-up by the normal Reveal rule or a card effect, then resolve any immediate result.
- Retrieve: effect-based recovery from discard, available only when explicitly granted by card text.
- Sacrifice: discard a card without placing for tactical advantage.
- Retainer Card: tactical card with unique abilities.
- Token: generic shorthand in card text for a Protection token unless a rule/card explicitly says otherwise.
- Trap: a face-down hidden Retainer effect that resolves when revealed, as printed on that card.
- Unprotected / Protected: a card without/with a current protection layer on its top active state. A Protection token, Market, Castle, or Capital on that top active state makes the card protected unless a rule/card explicitly says otherwise.
- Unique Special Retainer: unique retainer variant tied to a specific Daimyo.
- Spy: look at another player's hand.
- Steal: take token from another player's board card; this includes tokens on Daimyo cards when those tokens are on-board. If needed by effect, remaining tokens are taken from that player's Player Supply.
- Swap: exchange one card/token with another.
- Top Layer: uppermost visible card of a pile; non-top layers are untargetable by default unless an effect explicitly says otherwise.
- Valid Location: legal placement with no forbidden Numbered duplication in row/column/zone.
- Visit the Emperor: action representing imperial visit mechanic.
- Zone: 3x2 group of prefectures used for control and income checks.

## APPENDIX: RETAINERS (HISTORICAL NOTES & ACTIONS)

[HISTORICAL NOTE] Many Sengoku-era armies mixed elite samurai leadership with mass infantry (*ashigaru*), specialists, warrior monks, and irregular forces. Some terms below refer to real historical roles; others are simplified for gameplay.
[CARD DATA SOURCE] Action text in this appendix is aligned to `2026/PROJECTS/Sengoku-V25/data/data.json`, set `Reference_specials_rew_f`, as of 2026-04-23.
[TAG NOTE] Inferred action tags: [DISCARD] / [PLACE] / [PLACE_CHAINED] / [COVER] / [TRAP].
[TAG RULE] [PLACE_CHAINED] means the card must be placed near your Chain of Command, or cover a legal card as printed. "Return" means return to the relevant owner's hand unless a card says otherwise.

### Samurai — Elite Knight (#Army 1)

[HISTORICAL NOTE] Samurai were the warrior elite who served lords through military service, personal loyalty, and household retinues.

**Action1** [PLACE_CHAINED]/[COVER]: Place near your chain, or cover your Clan card, then Kill 1 unprotected adjacent card, or remove 1 Token or 1 Market.
> Action1 explanation: you must place this retainer near your chain, or cover one of your clan cards, then choose one immediate removal effect.

**Action2** [DISCARD]/[REACTION]: During an opponent's turn, discard to Cancel a declared Retainer card action before it resolves. Discard that card too. Samurai cannot block Trap effects.
> Action2 explanation: this is a reactive interrupt. It cancels the declared Retainer action and discards the cancelled card, but it does not stop Trap effects.

### Ashigaru — Light Infantry (#Army 2)

[HISTORICAL NOTE] *Ashigaru* were "light-foot" infantry, often recruited from commoners, and became the backbone of many Sengoku armies.

**Action1** [PLACE_CHAINED]/[COVER]: Place near your chain, or cover any own-clan unprotected Numbered card. Kill up to 2 adjacent unprotected Numbered or face-down cards.
> Action1 explanation: the printed clan tag changes by deck. The action can remove zero, one, or two valid adjacent targets.

**Action2** [DISCARD]: Draw 4 cards. Keep 1 and return the others together to the top or bottom of the deck.
> Action2 explanation: this is a deck-filter action; draw four, keep one, and place the other three together on either the top or bottom of the deck.

### Chajin — Tea Master (#Power 1)

[HISTORICAL NOTE] Tea culture became a political tool in late Sengoku Japan. Notably, the tea master Sen no Rikyū served both Oda Nobunaga and Toyotomi Hideyoshi, and tea gatherings could signal status and diplomacy.

**Action1** [DISCARD]: Draw 2 cards. Max 1 Chajin action per turn.
> Action1 explanation: this is a discard-to-draw effect with a printed per-turn limit.

**Action2** [DISCARD]: Draw 1 card. If it is a Retainer and you play it right away, its bonus becomes 3.
> Action2 explanation: the drawn card must be played immediately to receive the boosted Retainer bonus.

### Sohei — Warrior Monk (#Power 2)

[HISTORICAL NOTE] *Sōhei* (warrior monks) were armed forces linked to major temples. At times they acted as political players as well as fighters.

**Action1** [PLACE]/[COVER]: Place on a Sohei, unprotected Numbered card, or face-down card of any Clan.
> Action1 explanation: this is an overlap placement action. The target may belong to any clan if it matches the printed condition.

**Action2** [DISCARD]: Retrieve the top 1 card from your discard pile into your hand.
> Action2 explanation: this returns only the top card of your discard pile.

### Shinobi — Spy Assassin (#Mercenary 1)

[HISTORICAL NOTE] By the Sengoku period, Shinobi roles included espionage, scouting, infiltration, surprise attacks, and agitation/sabotage.

**Action1** [DISCARD]: Remove from the map 1 Retainer and any Token on it, or 1 face-down card.
> Action1 explanation: choose one target option and remove it from the map. This is one of the explicit printed exceptions that can affect a protected layer.

**Action2** [DISCARD]/[TRAP]: Spy on a player's hand. Choose 2 cards to discard. At the end of turn, draw +1 card. Trap affects whoever revealed it.
> Action2 explanation: inspect the chosen player's hand, force the printed discards, then apply the delayed end-of-turn draw. If revealed as a Trap, the effect hits the revealer.

### Ronin — Masterless Warrior (#Mercenary 2)

[HISTORICAL NOTE] A *rōnin* was a masterless samurai. In periods of upheaval, they could become dangerous free agents, switching sides or selling their skills.

**Action1** [PLACE]: Place on your own or any Clan's unprotected top [non-Ronin] card, replacing it. Return that card, then discard or return the rest of the stack.
> Action1 explanation: the target must be the unprotected top non-Ronin card in that space. Ronin remains in that space as the replacement card. If cards are returned, each returned card goes to its owner's hand.

**Action2** [DISCARD]/[TRAP]: Steal up to 2 Tokens from cards of other Clans. From the revealer first when it is a Trap. Max 1 per Clan; rest from supply.
> Action2 explanation: steal available board Protection tokens first, respecting the max-per-clan limit. If the printed amount cannot be fully taken from cards, complete from the relevant supply as printed.

### Unique Special Retainers

Each clan also has a Unique Special Retainer or an additional variant card. These vary by clan and should be read from the printed card. Known current V25 examples include Nyokan, Chonin, Hyakusho, and Kagemusha, plus some white-number variants of standard Retainers.
