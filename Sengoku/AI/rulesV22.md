## 1) GAME OVERVIEW
The game is set in the Sengoku period ("Warring States period"), described as a civil-war era in Japanese history (1467-1615). Players act as Daimyos, using strategy and deception to conquer territories, build structures, and leverage card effects.

Core arc:
- Build influence by placing Numbered and Special cards on a map-like board.
- Expand control by respecting Sudoku-like placement constraints.
- Use tokens to protect cards and evolve buildings.
- Use diplomacy (alliances) and tactical correction (error/rebellion system).
- End game is triggered when Shogun conditions are reached.

Victory statement in the rulebook:
- Winner is the player with the most points from military power and conquest.

### QUESTIONS (Game Overview)
QUESTION: Is the "simple to learn and addictive game" statement intended to imply a beginner mode not formally listed in this rulebook?
A: Yes there is a simple way of playing and an advance way of playing that implies: visit to the emperor, Alliances and a objective for each player to achieve to have extra points
QUESTION: "Final battle is ready to begin" is narrative wording; what exact mechanical condition defines this besides Shogun declaration?
A: This is a reference to the Sekigahara battle, in this game we want to become shogun and then the final winner is the player with more points (not the shogun that has certainly an advantage on points). The winner is the player that most likely will win the final battle (but it is not in this game that we fight the battle)
QUESTION: Is there any tie-break procedure when final points are equal?
A: Yes, two or more players can have the exact points at the end, in that case the winner is probably the player with more tokens reserved

## 6) SPECIAL CARDS ANATOMY
Special cards represent retainers sent to support influence or sacrificed for tactical effects.

General structure:
- Each Special has Action 1 and Action 2.
- Only one action can be activated before that card is discarded or placed.
- Activation mode depends on card icon:
  - Discard icon -> sacrifice to trigger effect.
  - Place icon -> place card on board to trigger effect.

Card classes:
- Army (Samurai, Ashigaru): destructive effects.
- Mercenaries (Ninja, Ronin): disruption and military weakening.
- Powered (Tea Master, Temple/Warrior Monk): enhance gameplay/card synergy.

Unique Special card:
- Gold-like layout + star symbol.
- Limited-power variant of another Special.
- Pair is Daimyo-specific.

RULE:
- Special cards can be combined with other Specials if power text allows.
- A Special card cannot be used twice in the same player's turn.
- Unique Special can never be combined with its twin/base counterpart.

NOTES from Daimyo Card panel:
- More than one player can become Shogun before game end.
- You may store additional tokens on Daimyo card up to 7 (spare tokens may be stolen by other players).

## 7) CARD PLACEMENT RULES
When placing from hand, follow card instructions plus Sudoku principles:

RULES:
- Daimyo cards cannot share row, column, or zone with other Daimyos.
- Numbered cards cannot duplicate within the same row, column, or zone (across all clans).
- Exception: Special cards are allowed in same row, column, or zone as other Special cards.

If no valid placement is possible:
- Discard one card to Discard Deck.
- End turn without placing cards.

Stacking rule:
- Players can place cards on top of their own Numbered cards already on board if placement is valid.
- This creates a pile that protects cards below.

Reference note in book:
- "for definition of zones, rows and columns see page xx" (placeholder unresolved in PDF).

TIP:
- When many cards cannot be placed validly, stacking Numbered cards can be a strategic fallback.

## 8) ERRORS - REBELLIONS
Immediate correction:
- If a player places an invalid card and others notice immediately, the card is withdrawn and move is reconsidered with no penalty.

Delayed detection ("rebellion"):
- If error is spotted after original turn, first player who declares it during their own turn may fix it.

RULE:
- Whenever you spot an error on your turn, you can fix it.

Fixing process:
- Spotting player can remove the duplicate card causing error (any clan, even own).
- Everything on that card is lost (tokens/buildings/etc).
- Player who fixes error gains 1 token (usable on board or storable on player board).

After correction:
- Check if game state is now valid and rebellion is stopped.

Treason concept:
- A player may intentionally place an error hoping it remains undetected until their or ally turn.

TIP:
- Revealing an error outside your turn may let another player claim correction reward.

TIP:
- Fixing errors can be weaponized to remove enemy defenses, castles, or capitals.

TIP:
- Treason can have high payoff but high risk.

## 9) CARDS AND TOKENS
Tokens placed on board cards become Power Tokens and support:
- protection,
- building conversion (Siege Towers, Castles, Capitals, etc).

Protected card rule:
- A card with a Power Token cannot be attacked or directly removed unless token is first removed, except where card actions say otherwise.

Ways token placement affects gameplay:
- Add 1 token to Numbered or Special card -> protection.
- Bring token count on Numbered/Special/Daimyo card to 2 -> create Siege Tower (discard those power tokens).
- On Daimyo card, up to 7 tokens can accumulate -> increases Daimyo Rank level.

Game terms included in this chapter:
- Adjacent cards: touch horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.
- Connected cards: touch horizontally or vertically only.
- Chain of Command: connected group including Daimyo; critical for defense evolution and scoring.

## 10) TOKENS TYPES
### Honour Tokens
- Represent honor and ability to perform specific actions (including alliances).
- Stored in Honour Tokens Reserve on Player Board, protected from stealing.
- Storage cap exists; extra tokens are lost/discarded.
- Book note: Daimyo has 3 Power Token on Player Board that are safe and cannot be lost.

### Power Tokens
- Any token placed on a board card becomes a Power Token.
- Protects card and forms foundation for structures.
- Effective token management is critical in advanced phases.
- Book caption example: 2 tokens on Daimyo card raise Rank to level 2.

NOTE:
- You cannot remove your own tokens from your Daimyo card.

### Alliance Tokens
- When alliance is formed, each player places one token of partner clan on their Daimyo card.
- Not spendable by players.
- Contribute to Daimyo honor/rank.
- Losing/removing alliance token breaks alliance immediately.

RULE:
- Alliance tokens are a pact symbol and cannot be used like normal power/honor spend.

NOTE:
- Token icon in descriptions is the hand fan symbol.

## 11) DEFENCES
Tokens are used to:
- protect cards,
- strengthen board position,
- build Siege Towers and Castles (and eventually Capitals).

Strategic distinction:
- Siege Towers: flexible defense + anti-defense offense.
- Castles: stronger defense + key evolution step toward Capitals.

### Building a Siege Tower
Requirement:
- 2 tokens on same card.

Process:
- Remove both tokens (back to containers).
- Place one Siege Tower on that card position.

RULE:
- No more than 1 tower can be built on a card at the same time, but card may still accept another token or different building.

Tower removal/attack functions:
- 1 tower sacrificed -> remove adjacent tower OR remove 1 token on adjacent card.
- 2 towers -> remove adjacent unprotected Numbered card.
- 3 towers -> remove adjacent Castle.
- 4 towers -> remove adjacent Capital.

RULE:
- Siege Towers do not count toward token count for creating Castles/Capitals.

NOTE:
- The tower has a specific symbol in game description.

## 12) BUILDING CASTLES
Requirement to build a Castle:
- Daimyo is Rank 1.
- 3 tokens are on cards in Chain of Command.
- At least one of those cards is Numbered.

Process:
- Remove those 3 tokens and return to supply.
- Place Castle on any undefended Numbered card in Chain of Command:
  - no power token,
  - no castle,
  - no capital.

Mandatory evolution clause:
- If the 3 power tokens are all on numeric cards in Chain of Command, they must evolve into a Castle (if rank condition met).
- If any of those 3 tokens are on Special card or Daimyo card, Castle can be built later.

NOTE:
- You can never have more than 3 Castles on the board at any time.

TIP:
- Castle strongly increases card defense and Daimyo income.

## 16) ALLIANCES
Eligibility:
- Alliances only possible with at least 3 players.

Formation process:
- Active player asks one other player during Alliance phase (after card play).
- Target player accepts or declines.
- If declined, proposer must wait another turn before requesting alliance again.
- If accepted:
  - alliance is formed,
  - both players receive bonus listed on their boards,
  - both exchange and place Alliance Token on Daimyo cards.

Limits:
- 3 to 5 players: only one alliance can exist between two Daimyos.
- More than 5 players: max 2 alliances per Daimyo.

RULE:
- During alliance, players cannot use Special Card actions against each other.
- Numbered card placement remains unrestricted except normal validity.

Alliance token effects:
- Not usable for Castle/building creation.
- Contributes to Daimyo ranking.
- If alliance token is lost/removed by another player's action, alliance breaks immediately.

Breaking alliance:
- Can be declared on player's turn after card play.
- Return alliance token to other player's container.
- Other ally replaces alliance token with a power token.
- Alliances cannot be broken on the same turn they are formed.
- Daimyo rank cannot downgrade, so player must ensure an Honour token is available on player board for replacement logic.

TIP:
- Allies can coordinate on hidden errors and joint tactical correction.

NOTE:
- Some Daimyos gain benefits from breaking alliance (historical traitor flavor).

## 18) TURN STRUCTURE
Rulebook states 4 main phases:
1. Draw Phase
2. Action Phase
3. Alliance Phase
4. Collection Phase (end of turn)

Action Phase options (order flexible):
- Play cards (Numbered or Special) until only 2 cards remain in hand.
- Use tokens from Daimyo or Player Board to protect cards/build Siege Towers/Castles/Capitals.
- Declare and resolve errors.
- Visit the Emperor (reshuffle/redraw).

Alliance Phase:
- Make new alliance or break existing one.

Collection Phase:
- Base income: 1 token.
- +1 each conquered Zone.
- +1 each complete row or column.
- +1 each Castle.
- +2 each Capital.
- Place all gained tokens in Honour Tokens Reserve.

RULE:
- Player board storage limit is 5 tokens; excess discarded.

Draw text appears again at end:
- "Finally the player draws up to 3 cards... Turn ends and passes clockwise."

RULE:
- If deck runs out, reshuffle and place face down as new draw deck; allows reused Special cards.

## 20) FINAL SCORING (STANDARD)
Scoring references Chain of Command (cards connected orthogonally to Daimyo).

Score:
- +1 each Numbered or Special card on board.
- +1 each full zone or line under control.

## 21) LET'S START! PREPARATION PHASE - JUNBI
Round structure:
- Game consists of rounds; each round has player turns split into phases.

Preparation phase steps:
1. Select first player by Order Number on Player Board (lowest starts; historical flavor note: Daimyo who died before others).
2. First player places Daimyo card in a starting area marked by clan symbol.
3. Place Daimyo in any space within that area (starting Prefecture indicator).
4. Place one Numbered card from hand next to Daimyo.
5. Draw back up to 3 cards.
6. Next player repeats, with additional Daimyo placement restriction:
   - cannot place Daimyo in same row, column, or zone as previous Daimyo.
7. Continue until all players placed Daimyo and first Numbered card.
8. Startup completes; play passes clockwise back to first player.

NOTE:
- If a player has no Numbered card in hand during setup, reshuffle full hand back into deck and redraw 3 (may repeat multiple times).

## 23) RULE CHANGES (DUELS)
### Chain of power
- Cards placed must be connected to Chain of power (must link back to Daimyo card).
- Disconnected cards lose power.
- Disconnected Castle/Capital no longer provide income.
- Entire disconnected zone also provides no income.

NOTE:
- Connected cards are horizontal/vertical neighbors only.

### The Bridge: center zone
- Central zone gives +1 income to Daimyo who controls it and has it in chain.
- No Tokens, Siege Towers, or Buildings can be placed on this space.
- Numbered and Special cards can be placed normally.

### Seppuku and Honour
- Once per game, a player's general may commit Seppuku.
- Pay 1 token and discard wakizashi.
- Collect all cards into full deck, shuffle, draw 3.

NOTE:
- Text says this replaces the visit to Emperor in the 3-4 player game.

## 26) GLOSSARY
