## 1) Components (Standard set, 4 players)
- 1 main board (6×6 for 3–4 players) + Duel board (5×5) if included.
- 4 player boards.
- 4 clan decks (**20 cards each**):
  - 1 Daimyō
  - 12 Numbered cards (1–6, two copies each)
  - 6 Retainers
  - 1 Unique Special Retainer
- Koku tokens: **36** (9 per player).
- Protection tokens: **20** (5 per player).
- Markets: **4** (1 per player).
- Castles: **12** (3 per player).
- Capitals: **4** (1 per player).
- Emperor Visit markers: **4** (Expert Mode only).

Hard limits:
- Max **9 Koku** stored.
- Max **1 Market** per player.
- Max **3 Castles** per player.
- Max **1 active Capital** for Shogun progression.

---

## 2) Terms (quick glossary)
(Full glossary in **Section 19**.)
- **Koku (K#)**: currency stored on the player board. **Cap 9**.
- **Protection token**: token placed on the map for defense and building progress.
- **Chain of Command**: orthogonally connected group that includes your Daimyō.
- **Adjacent**: orthogonal or diagonal.
- **Connected**: orthogonal only.
- **Imperial Spaces**: 4 marked central spaces with Emperor icon.
- **Zone**: one of the 6 main areas (Z1–Z6 on 6×6).
- **Line**: a full row or column spanning one side of the board to the opposite side.

---

## 3) Setup (Standard)
1. Choose a clan color; take player board + pieces.
2. Separate the **Daimyō** card from your deck.
3. Shuffle the remaining 19 cards; draw **3**.
4. Place your Daimyō on the map in a legal starting prefecture (**corners are not valid**).
5. Choose first player; proceed clockwise.

Correction: if the Daimyō is accidentally drawn in setup, reveal it, move it to the player board, then draw a replacement card.

---

## 4) Board, control, and income sources
### 4.1 Control rules
- Control checks use the **top visible** card in a stack.
- Face-down cards count as your clan for control, but do not score as cards.

### 4.2 Zones, Lines, Imperial Spaces
- Zones: full control grants income and endgame points.
- Lines: full control grants income and endgame points.
- Imperial Spaces: each controlled Imperial Space grants **+1 Koku** during Collection.

Rule: a prefecture can contribute to both a Zone and a Line; if you satisfy both control conditions, **both contributions count**.

---

## 5) Turn structure (timing)
A turn has three sections.

### 5.1 Beginning of turn
- Draw up to **3 cards**.

### 5.2 Main section (order is flexible unless a rule says otherwise)
You may do, in any sequence:
- Play cards (until you stop at **1–2 cards** in hand).
- Convert Koku → Protection tokens (K2 → 1 token), any amount.
- Place Protection tokens.
- Build Market/Castle/Capital when conditions are met.
- Reveal (Bribery): pay **K3** to reveal any face-down card (including your own).
- Fix Errors (Rebellions).
- Declare Shogun when valid.
- Expert Mode only: Alliances, Visit the Emperor, Destiny Path steps.

Hand-floor clarification:
- You normally stop after playing down to **2 cards**, then you may choose to play one more and end with **1**.
- You are never forced to spend your last remaining card.

### 5.3 End of turn
- Draw back up to **3 cards**.
- Collect Koku income (Player Board is the value source of truth).
- Discard excess above cap (K9).
- Pass turn.

---

## 6) Economy
### 6.1 Spend Koku
- **Reveal** a face-down card: **K3**.
- **Convert** to Protection tokens: **K2 → 1 token** (any amount during your main section).

### 6.2 Gain Koku
- Error fixing rewards: **+1 Koku per Error fixed**.
- End-of-turn income: Base + bonuses (Market, Zones, Lines, Imperial Spaces).  
  (Income sources stack; excess above cap is discarded.)

---

## 7) Cards
### 7.1 Daimyō
- Placed at setup; remains on the board.
- Starts your Chain of Command.
- Holds up to **3 reserved build tokens**.
- Does **not** score as a card at endgame.

### 7.2 Numbered cards (1–6)
- Must always respect Sudoku validity when face-up.
- Main bases for Protection and Buildings.
- Can stack if permitted and still valid.
- May be placed face-down as placeholders (see Face-down rules).

### 7.3 Retainers
- Tactical cards with **A1/A2** choice.
- Use mode is defined by icon/text (Place vs Discard).
- Unique Special Retainer follows its printed icon logic.

---

## 8) Placement rules (Sudoku + exceptions)
- Numbered cards must always be placed in a way that keeps the map **valid**.
- Retainers placed “anywhere” ignore Sudoku validity restrictions unless their text says otherwise.
- In stacks, the top visible layer determines legality and most targeting.

---

## 9) Face-down cards and Reveal (Bribery)
### 9.1 Face-down placement
- By default, only **Numbered** cards may be placed face-down as hidden placeholders.
- Some Retainers may be placed face-down as traps (current set includes Shinobi/Ronin; expansions may add more).
- Face-down cards cannot receive Protection tokens.
- Face-down cards count for control (if yours) but do not score as cards.

Accidental face-down:
- If a non-allowed card is placed face-down by mistake, flip it face-up immediately (no cost) and continue play.

### 9.2 Reveal action
- Pay **K3** to reveal any face-down card (including your own).
- If revealed card is a trap: resolve its trap effect once, then remove it from the map and discard it.
- Reveal may create, expose, or resolve Errors depending on what becomes face-up.

### 9.3 Covering a face-down card (stacks)
- You may **cover** a face-down card by placing a face-up card on top of it.
- While a face-down card is **not** the **top visible** layer, it is considered **hidden inside a stack**:
  - it cannot be targeted by default,
  - and it cannot be **Revealed** unless it becomes the top layer (unless an effect explicitly targets deeper layers or the entire stack).
- The top visible card remains the **primary target** for attacks and removals (unless a card effect says otherwise — e.g., Ronin can affect a whole stack).


---

## 10) Errors (Rebellions)
- An Error is an illegal Sudoku state (typically duplicates in row/column/zone).
- If spotted during the acting player’s turn, any player may call it before the turn passes.
- On your turn, you may fix any detected Errors.
- The fixer chooses which offending card to remove and the order.
- Each Error fixed grants **+1 Koku**.
- Removing an offending protected layer removes its attached Protection token/building with it.

---

## 11) Protection and buildings
### 11.1 Protection tokens
- Placeable only on eligible **face-up** cards.
- Max one piece per card: **1 token OR 1 Castle OR 1 Market**.
- Protected cards cannot be removed unless protection is removed first, or an effect explicitly bypasses it.
- In stacks, protection applies to the **top** visible layer.

### 11.2 Player supply and stolen tokens
Each player keeps a personal **supply pile** next to their board.

Steal rule (color conversion):
- If an effect steals **Protection tokens**:
  1) remove tokens from the target (**map first**, then their **supply** if needed),
  2) replace them with the attacker’s color tokens as required,
  3) place them as instructed by the effect.
- **Never** take Koku when stealing Protection tokens. Koku stays on the player board.
- Protection tokens are a **map-only** resource: you can’t convert them back into Koku.

### 11.3 Build a Castle or Market
Requirement:
- Commit **3 Protection tokens** in your Chain of Command.

Process:
1. Remove the 3 committed tokens.
2. Place **1 Castle** or **1 Market** on an eligible connected Numbered card.


Building bases:
- **Castles** and **Markets** can be placed only on **face-up Numbered cards**.
- You may protect some Retainers (if eligible), but you can **never** place a Castle/Market/Capital on top of a Retainer.

Notes:
- Max **1 Market** per player.
- Market behaves as one protection layer and can be removed by legal effects or by Error correction.
- Castles are typically removed only if their layer is removed by Error correction, unless a card explicitly removes a Castle.

Mandatory transformation:
- If all 3 committed tokens are on connected Numbered cards, conversion triggers immediately when the third token is placed.
- If tokens are split across Daimyō/Retainer/Numbered cards, conversion may wait until a valid Numbered destination is available.

### 11.4 Capital
Requirements:
- **3 Castles** in your Chain of Command.
- Continuous connection to your Daimyō.

Process:
1. Remove the 3 Castles.
2. Place 1 Capital on a connected Numbered base card that is face-down:
   - If the base is face-up, flip it face-down first.
   - If a face-down card already exists there (including a hidden trap), use it as the base.

---

## 12) Attacks
Attacks happen through:
- Retainer actions
- Error correction removals
- Trap/Reveal outcomes

Resolve effects sequentially, one at a time.

Voluntary sacrifice:
- On your turn, you may remove your own Castle or Capital and return the piece to supply.

---

## 13) Shogun and endgame (Standard)
- If you have **1 connected Capital**, you may declare Shogun during your turn.
- The first Shogun is the anchor; all other players take one final turn (Final Cycle).
- Other players may also declare Shogun during the Final Cycle.
- If your Capital becomes disconnected before closure, your Shogun status is not valid until reconnected.
- Expert Mode: all alliances end immediately when the Final Cycle begins.

Anchor edge case:
- If the anchor player loses Shogun status before closure and later restores it, they must declare again and wait one full rotation to close the game.

---

## 14) Scoring (Standard)
Score **connected** elements only:

- **+1** each face-up card on board (Numbered or Retainer).
- **+1** each fully controlled Zone or Line.
- **+1** each Castle.
- **+2** each Capital.
- **+4** if you are Shogun.
- **+1** (Expert Mode) if you never used the Emperor reshuffle right.

---

## 15) Expert Mode modules (summary)
### 15.1 Alliances
- Alliances form per player-board Expert conditions.
- Alliance token = Protection token of ally’s color placed on Daimyō.
- While it is an alliance marker it is not spendable like a normal token.
- If removed, the alliance ends immediately.
- In 5–6 player Expert Mode, each player may maintain max **2** alliances.

### 15.2 Visit the Emperor
- One reshuffle right per match.
- Marker tracks usage on player board.
- Can be declared on your turn after drawing and before playing cards.
- On first deck exhaustion (if unused), perform Emperor reshuffle; no second reshuffle.

---

## 16) Preparation Phase — Junbi (optional narrative)
Junbi is the initial placement phase used to establish starting Daimyo positions.

Preparation steps:
1. Determine first player.
2. First player places Daimyo on a legal starting edge location.
3. Next players place Daimyo clockwise, each respecting Daimyo Sudoku restrictions (no same row, column, or zone conflict with already placed Daimyo cards).
4. Once all Daimyo cards are placed, normal turn flow begins.

Setup clarification:
- This preparation phase uses Daimyo placement only.
- Numbered-card setup placement is not part of this phase in the current ruleset.

Starting locations:
- Starting Daimyo locations are specific edge positions marked for setup.
- Coordinate reference (6x6): B1, D1, C1, E1, A2, F2, A3, F3, A4, F4, A5, F5, B6, C6, D6, E6.
- Coordinates for 5x5 (Duels) and 9x9 setups are different and should be documented in their dedicated setup diagrams.
- Only the listed starting locations are valid; corner prefectures are not valid starting positions.

Fallback rule:
- If a player must redraw for setup-hand constraints, reshuffle and redraw until at least one valid Numbered card is available for normal play.

## 17) Duels (2 players)
Sengoku Duels is the dedicated 2-player mode and remains part of the game.

Board and setup:
- Use the 5x5 Duels board ("The Challenge").
- Each player selects one clan and prepares deck/components.
- Separate Daimyo card, shuffle remaining deck, then draw 3 cards.
- If the starting hand has 3 Retainer cards, reshuffle and redraw.

Opening placement:
- First player places Daimyo in a legal starting space, then places one connected Numbered card.
- Second player does the same, respecting Daimyo placement restrictions (no same row/column/zone conflict with first Daimyo).

RULE:
- Duels uses the same card-driven offense model as Standard mode.

ILLUSTRATION: 5x5 Duels board with starting slots and central bridge area.

---

Duels mode uses tighter connectivity and board-pressure rules.

Chain of Command:
- Cards must remain connected to the Daimyo chain to stay fully active.
- Disconnected cards lose scoring and tactical value until reconnected.
- Disconnected buildings do not provide income/scoring while disconnected.

Bridge (center space) rule:
- The center bridge zone grants +1 income when controlled and connected.
- Cards may be placed there if legal.
- Buildings cannot be placed on the bridge space.

Economy and actions:
- Core Koku/protection rules remain active.
- Expert-only modules (Alliances / Visit the Emperor) are optional and should be enabled only if both players agree before setup.

RULE:
- Duels follows the same attack-resolution order defined in Section 14.
- Disconnected cards lose chain-based effects immediately at disconnection.

ILLUSTRATION: Duels rule panel with chain and bridge examples.


---

Duels endgame follows the same Shogun trigger principle used in standard mode.

Trigger:
- A player declares Shogun when they satisfy the active Capital condition with valid chain connectivity.

Closure:
- After declaration, the opponent takes one final turn.
- If the opponent also satisfies Shogun conditions in that final turn, both statuses are considered during scoring.
- After that final turn, the game ends and scoring begins.

ILLUSTRATION: Duels endgame timeline from first declaration to final scoring.
RULE:
- If both players are Shogun at closure, first tie-break is the player with more tokens on the board.
- If still tied, second tie-break is the player with more remaining Koku.
- If still tied, the result is a shared victory.


---

Duels scoring is based on connected control and marked board value.

Scoring items:
- +1 or +2 for Numbered/Retainer cards on marked scoring spaces (as printed on Duels board).
- +1 for each complete controlled zone/line (if connected to chain).
- +1 for controlling the central bridge zone.
- +1 for each Castle.
- +2 for each Capital.
- +4 for each Shogun declaration.
- +1 if (Expert Mode) the player never used their Emperor reshuffle right.

RULE:
- Only connected cards/structures count unless a specific effect states otherwise.

TIP:
- In Duels, board position and chain continuity usually score more efficiently than overbuilding.

ILLUSTRATION: Duels scoring map with marked spaces and bridge bonus highlighted.

RULE:
- +1 and +2 Duels scoring spaces are the marked spaces printed directly on the Duels board.

## 19) Full glossary
Glossary terms should reflect current economy split and reveal cost in Koku (K3).

Extracted keywords:
- Adjacent/Nearby/Near/Next: side or corner contact (orthogonal + diagonal).
- Add to the effect: increase strength of another card ability.
- Alliance Token: marks alliance on Daimyo card.
- Chain: connected card group; "Chain of Command" when Daimyo included.
- Clan: faction of Daimyo; all player cards belong to same clan.
- Connected: side contact only, no diagonal.
- Daimyo: Japanese lord character represented by player.
- Discard/Eliminate/Kill: remove card from play to owner's discard pile.
Bribery/Reveal: paying Koku to flip a face-down card face-up.
Cover: place a card on top of another card (creating a stack). The top visible layer becomes the primary target.
Face-down: a hidden card state. Face-down cards count for control but do not score as cards.
Final Cycle (Shogunate Final Cycle): the last round triggered by the first Shogun declaration.
Hand-floor: the usual minimum hand size you stop at when playing cards (normally 2; you may choose to go to 1).
Stack: multiple cards in one prefecture. By default only the top visible layer is targetable.
Trap: a face-down Retainer that triggers an effect when revealed.
Reveal: flip a face-down card face-up by paying Koku.
- Emperor: non-playing sovereign enabling the Expert-mode reshuffle flow through its dedicated cost/marker rule.
- Error: same Numbered cards in same row/column/zone; must be fixed.
- Game Board: board where cards are placed.
- Honour Tokens: honor resource in Player Board reserve.
- Interrupt: stop/nullify opponent card effect instantly.
- Koku: physical currency token used on the player board to pay actions and conversions.
- Market: economic building that grants +K1 income during Collection while controlled.
- Numbered Card: number-identified prefecture/province card.
- Place: set a card on board location.
- Player Board: unique personal board with info and token spaces.
- Player Supply: each player's off-map component pool next to the Player Board.
- Power Token: token on board card used for defense/buildings.
- Prefecture: single board space.
- Retrieve: effect-based recovery from discard, available only when explicitly granted by card text.
- Sacrifice: discard a card without placing for tactical advantage.
- Retainer Card: tactical card with unique abilities.
- Unique Special Retainer: unique retainer variant tied to a specific Daimyo.
- Spy: look at another player's hand.
- Steal: take token from another player's board card; this includes tokens on Daimyo cards when those tokens are on-board. If needed by effect, remaining tokens are taken from that player's Player Supply.
- Swap: exchange one card/token with another.
- Top Layer: uppermost visible card of a pile; non-top layers are untargetable by default unless an effect explicitly says otherwise.
- Valid Location: legal placement with no forbidden Numbered duplication in row/column/zone.
- Visit the Emperor: action representing imperial visit mechanic.
- Zone: 3x2 group of prefectures used for control and income checks.

## Appendix A — Retainers Actions (v24 snapshot)
> Text is a snapshot; always obey the final card text if a mismatch exists.

### Samurai — Elite Knight (#Army 1)
- **A1:** Place *near* or Cover a card of your *Clan*, then Kill **1** *unprotected* *adjacent* card **or** Remove 1 Token  
- **A2:** During an opponent's turn, Block their *Special* card action. (They may use another card.)

### Ashigaru — Light Infantry (#Army 2)
- **A1:** Place *near* a card of your *Clan*. Kill up to **2** *adjacent* *unprotected* *Number* or *face-down* cards  
- **A2:** Draw **4** cards, keep **1**, return the others to top/bottom of the deck.

### Chajin — Tea Master (#Power 1)
- **A1:** Draw 2 new cards (max 1 of this Chajin action per turn).  
- **A2:** Draw and play 1 card immediately. If it’s a *Special*, add **+2** to the effect (max **3**).

### Sohei — Warrior Monk (#Power 2)
- **A1:** Place on top of another Sohei or *unprotected* *Numbered* or *face-down* card of any clan.  
- **A2:** Retrieve the top **1** card from the discard pile into your hand.

### Shinobi — Spy Assassin (#Mercenary 1)
- **A1:** Kill and remove from the map 1 *Special* + any Token on it, **or** 1 *face-down* card.  
- **A2:** Spy on a player’s hand: pick **2** cards to discard, then draw *text+1* extra card at end of turn.

### Ronin — Masterless Warrior (#Mercenary 2)
- **A1:** Replace 1 *unprotected* (non-Ronin) card and return it to the owner; kill or return the rest of the stack.  
- **A2:** Steal up to **2** tokens from *Number* or *Retainer* cards (max 1 per player; rest from resources).

## Appendix B — [HISTORICAL NOTE] index (optional)
- [HISTORICAL NOTE] The Sengoku period (“Warring States”) was defined by constant conflict and rapid power shifts. The idea of *gekokujō* (“the low overcomes the high”) captured how underlings could overthrow superiors in this chaos.
- [HISTORICAL NOTE] *Koku* (石) was a real unit tied to rice yield (about 180 liters). It was widely used to measure wealth and a domain’s productivity.
- [HISTORICAL NOTE] A lord was typically considered daimyō-class at 10,000 koku or more.
- [HISTORICAL NOTE] During the Sengoku era, castles became administrative and military hubs. Castle towns (*jōkamachi*) grew around them as concentrated centers of power, trade, and logistics.
- [HISTORICAL NOTE] The title *Seii Taishōgun* was formally bestowed by the Emperor, providing imperial legitimacy to a military ruler (even when real power was contested).
- [HISTORICAL NOTE] Example: in 1603 Tokugawa Ieyasu received the shogun title from Emperor Go-Yōzei, often treated as the start of the Tokugawa shogunate.
- [HISTORICAL NOTE] Sengoku politics relied heavily on shifting coalitions. Alliances were often pragmatic and temporary, and could be reinforced through diplomacy (e.g., negotiated pledges and marriage ties).
- [HISTORICAL NOTE] The Imperial Court in Kyoto remained a symbolic source of legitimacy. Warlords often sought court titles or imperial recognition to strengthen their claims.
- [HISTORICAL NOTE] Example: Toyotomi Hideyoshi pursued court legitimacy and became *kampaku* (Imperial Regent) in 1585.
- [HISTORICAL NOTE] Many Sengoku-era armies mixed elite samurai leadership with mass infantry (*ashigaru*), specialists, and irregular forces. Some terms below refer to real historical roles; others are simplified for gameplay.
- [HISTORICAL NOTE] Samurai were the warrior elite who served lords through military service, personal loyalty, and household retinues.
- [HISTORICAL NOTE] *Ashigaru* were “light-foot” infantry, often recruited from commoners, and became the backbone of many Sengoku armies.
- [HISTORICAL NOTE] Tea culture became a political tool in late Sengoku Japan. Notably, the tea master Sen no Rikyū served both Oda Nobunaga and Toyotomi Hideyoshi, and tea gatherings could signal status and diplomacy.
- [HISTORICAL NOTE] *Sōhei* (warrior monks) were armed forces linked to major temples. At times they acted as political players as well as fighters.
- [HISTORICAL NOTE] By the Sengoku period, shinobi roles included espionage, scouting, infiltration, surprise attacks, and agitation/sabotage.
- [HISTORICAL NOTE] A *rōnin* was a masterless samurai. In periods of upheaval, they could become dangerous free agents, switching sides or selling their skills.
