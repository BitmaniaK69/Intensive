## 1) GAME OVERVIEW
Set during Japan's Sengoku period (1467-1615), the game places each player in command of a Daimyo clan competing for influence, territorial control, and political supremacy.

[HISTORICAL NOTE] The campaign focus points toward Sekigahara (1600), the decisive battle that determined the end of the Sengoku power struggle.
Daimyo were regional lords governing domains across Japan. Domain size varied widely (small, medium, and large), and the map's province/prefecture spaces are a gameplay abstraction rather than modern prefectural borders.

Players shape the map by placing Numbered and Retainer cards, creating tactical positions, and timing actions carefully. Koku is the game currency, while Protection tokens are map resources used for defense and building progression.

The game is played in two modes:
- Standard Mode: core rules focused on placement, control, defense, and scoring.
- Expert Mode: adds Alliances, Visit the Emperor, and Destiny Path objectives.

Core arc:
- Build influence by placing Numbered and Retainer cards on the board.
- Expand control by respecting Sudoku-like placement constraints.
- Use Protection tokens to defend key cards and convert progress into buildings.
- Push toward Capital formation and Shogun declaration.

Victory statement:
- The winner is the player with the highest final score.

RULE:
- If two or more players tie on score, the player with the highest remaining Koku on the Player Board wins.
- If still tied, tied players share the victory.

## 2) GAME COMPONENTS
The base game is designed for 4 players. Expanded setups (5-6 players) require the larger board and the additional clan components.

Listed components (base, 4 players):
- 1 main game board.
- 4 player boards.
- 4 player decks (20 cards each: 12 Numbered, 6 Retainer cards, 1 Extra Special Retainer, 1 Daimyo).
- 36 Koku tokens (9 per player).
- 20 player Protection Tokens (5 per player).
- 4 Emperor Visit markers (1 per player, used in Expert Mode).
- 4 Markets (1 per player).
- 12 Castles (3 per player).
- 4 Capitals (1 per player).

Player-color reference (base + expansions):
- Oda Nobunaga: dark red.
- Toyotomi Hideyoshi: green.
- Akechi Mitsuhide: blue.
- Tokugawa Ieyasu: yellow.

Expansion set:
- Date Masamune: violet.
- Mori Terumoto: light grey.
- Ishida Mitsunari: black.
- Lady Oichi: orange.

RULE:
- Protection tokens are map resources for defense/build progression.
- Koku is represented by physical Koku tokens, kept on the player board area and never placed on the map.
- Emperor Visit markers start standing on the player board.

RULE:
- Piece limits are hard limits (for example: max 3 Castles per player, max 1 active Capital in the current ruleset).

## 3) SETUP
Setup remains clan/deck based, with the Daimyo card separated before the first draw.

Before play:
1. Each player chooses a Daimyo/clan color.
2. Each player takes their board, deck, and components.
3. Place each Emperor Visit marker standing in its available space (Expert Mode tracking).

Deck preparation:
1. Remove the Daimyo card from the deck and keep it face up beside the Player Board (it will be placed on the Game Board during Junbi).
   - The Daimyo is parked beside/on the Player Board during setup only; it is not yet on the Game Board.
2. Shuffle the remaining 19 cards and place them face down on the side of the Player Board.
3. Draw an initial hand of 3 cards.
4. If the initial hand contains no Numbered cards, reshuffle that hand into the deck and redraw 3 cards (repeat until at least one Numbered card is in hand).

RULE:
- This setup redraw procedure is part of setup and is not Visit the Emperor (it does not consume/flip the Emperor marker).


Turn order:
- Determine only the first player.
- Play then proceeds clockwise.
- "Order Number" on player boards is guidance/theme and not a mandatory seating rule.
- On the 6x6 board, only the listed starting locations are valid; corner prefectures are not valid starting positions.

## 4) GAME ELEMENTS
Board and zone control drive both position and income.

### Game Board
Board formats:
- 2 players: 5x5 Duels board (with a shared central bridge space).
- 3-4 players: 6x6 board.
- 5-6 players: 9x9 board.

Zones:
- On the 6x6 board, Standard setup uses 6 zones (Z1-Z6), each as a 3x2 area.
- Zone geometry for 5x5 (The Duel) and 9x9 (The Grand Campaign) is pending definition and tracked in NOTE TO TEST/ADD.
- Zones are used for control checks and income.
- Imperial Zones are central marked spaces; each controlled Imperial Zone grants +1 Koku during Collection.
- There are four Imperial Zones in the center, marked with the Emperor icon; they cannot receive face-down cards, protection tokens, or any building.

Line control:
- A valid line runs from one side of the board to the opposite side.
- A line is controlled only if all its spaces are occupied by your clan (top cards; face-down top cards still count as their clan).
- Vertical and horizontal lines are valid.
- Diagonals are not valid lines.

Control and stacking:
- In stacked spaces, only the top card counts for control checks; if it is face-down, it still counts as its clan but has no revealed number.
- A prefecture can contribute to both its zone and a valid line at the same time.
- When both apply, both income contributions are counted.

### Player Board
Each player has a personal board used to manage information and resources during play.

It shows:
- Daimyo history and quick references.
- Turn-order number reference.
- Koku/resource tracking spaces.
- Expert-mode references (when enabled).
- Clan identity and symbols.

RULE:
- The Player Board repeats economy values as a quick reference.
- This rulebook defines the full economy timing, caps, and edge cases.
Player Board income schema (as shown):
- Every counting: +1 Koku (base income).
- With Market built: +1 Koku.
- Every Zone or Line conquered: +1 Koku (each).
- Per Central controlled (Imperial Zone): +1 Koku (each).
- End-of-round counting cap: max 5 Koku gained from this counting step.
- Storage cap is applied after counting: keep up to 6 Koku in storage, discard excess.

## 5) THE DECK AND CARD TYPES
Each Daimyo deck has 20 cards:
- 1 Daimyo card
- 12 Numbered cards (1-6, two copies each)
- 6 Retainer cards
- 1 Unique Special Retainer

Deck consistency:
- Numbered cards are the same across clans.
- Clan identity is shown by color/symbol styling.
- Numbered cards and Retainer cards share the same clan back style so hidden cards are not role-revealed from the back.

### Daimyo Card
- Placed on the Game Board during Junbi and remains on board.
- Starts the Chain of Command.
- Can hold up to 3 reserved tokens for later building use.
- Is not counted for end-game card scoring.

### Numbered Cards
- Represent prefectures/provinces.
- Used for control, stacking, and building destinations (when eligible).

### Retainer Cards
- Provide tactical effects through Action 1 / Action 2 choices.
- Use mode is strict by iconography/text:
  - Place-type action -> played onto board as defined.
  - Sacrifice/Discard-type action -> resolve then discard face up.

RULE:
- Unique Special Retainer follows its printed icon logic; place and discard modes are not interchangeable unless explicitly stated.

## 6) RETAINER CARDS ANATOMY
Retainer cards provide tactical actions and can be played either by placing them or by resolving their effect according to icon/text.

General structure:
- Each Retainer has 2 Order Actions, but only one can be chosen when the card is used.
- Use mode depends on icon/text (Place or Discard logic).

Action and usage rules:
- When you play a Retainer, choose and resolve exactly 1 of its 2 Order Actions.
- If that same Retainer card later returns to your hand, it may be played again and you may choose either Order Action again.
- Retainer effects are single-use unless card text explicitly states an exception.
- Some cards explicitly restrict repeated use in the same turn (for example specific Chajin text).
- A player may stop at 2 cards in hand; to play one final card (down to 1), they must pay 1 Koku.
- Reveal, Convert Koku, Build, and Error Fix are Turn Procedures (not Retainer Order Actions) and may all be performed in the same turn if legal.

Face-down cards (Numbered placeholders + hidden traps):
- Only **Numbered** cards may be placed face-down as hidden placeholders.
- Some **Retainers** cards such as Shinobi and Ronin may be placed face-down as hidden traps.
- A face-down **Numbered** card is a hidden placeholder with no active number while it remains face-down.
- While face-down, a Numbered placeholder does not create Numbered Sudoku conflicts (row/column/zone duplicates).
- While face-down on top of a space/stack, it still occupies the space and counts as its clan for control checks.
- Face-down cards cannot be placed in **Imperial Zones**.
- Face-down cards can be placed on top of **Retainer/Special** cards when stacking is otherwise legal.
- Face-down traps are not identifiable until a player pays the Reveal cost.
- No player (including the owner) may look at a face-down card without paying the Reveal cost, unless a card effect explicitly allows it.
- When a face-down card is revealed, flip it face-up and apply its normal identity immediately.
- If a revealed Numbered card now creates a forbidden duplicate, an Error is created immediately.
- When a face-down Shinobi or Ronin is revealed, resolve its trap effect once, then remove the card from the map and discard it.
- If any other card is accidentally placed face-down, flip it face-up immediately (no cost) and continue play.
- Face-down cards cannot receive Protection tokens.
- A Capital is always placed on a face-down base card (already face-down, or turned face-down during placement).
- While the Capital remains on top, the base card below cannot be attacked, revealed, or destroyed by effects.

Token-steal clarification:
- Token stealing targets tokens currently on the board (including tokens on Daimyo cards if present there).
- If a card effect says "1 from each clan" and the board has fewer valid targets, missing tokens are taken from that player's personal supply.
- Stolen Protection tokens are immediately placed/used as allowed by the stealing effect and are never converted into Koku.

NOTE:
- Keep a complete Retainer reference appendix aligned with the current card set to avoid text ambiguity during play.
RULE:
- The Retainer appendix is included in the final printable rulebook and placed at the end.

## 7) CARD PLACEMENT RULES
When placing from hand, follow both card text and Sudoku constraints.

RULES:
- Daimyo cards cannot share row, column, or zone with other Daimyos.
- Numbered cards cannot duplicate within the same row, column, or zone.
- Face-down Numbered placeholders have no active number while hidden, so they do not create Numbered duplicates until revealed.
- Retainer cards may coexist with other Retainer cards in the same row/column/zone unless card text says otherwise.

If no valid placement is possible:
- Discard one card.
- Continue the turn with any other legal non-placement actions.

Stacking:
- Stacking is allowed on Numbered cards, Retainer cards, face-down Numbered placeholders, and face-down Shinobi/Ronin traps.
- In a stack, only the top card is active for control/sudoku checks; if the top card is face-down, only clan ownership applies until reveal.
- If the top card is removed, the revealed lower card becomes active immediately.
- Revealed lower cards can immediately create errors that must be resolved.
- There is no hard limit on pile height.

TIP:
- Use stacking to preserve chain presence when open placement is blocked.

## 8) ERRORS - REBELLIONS

[HISTORICAL NOTE] The Sengoku period (“Warring States”) was defined by constant conflict and rapid power shifts. The idea of *gekokujō* (“the low overcomes the high”) captured how underlings could overthrow superiors in this chaos.

Errors are illegal board states (usually Numbered duplicates in a forbidden row/column/zone) and can be exploited tactically if not corrected in time.

Immediate detection:
- If an illegal placement is detected during the acting player's turn, any player may call it before turn passes.
- In this immediate timing window, the acting player must correct the placement at once (move the card to a legal location or choose another legal play/card).
- If the acting player changes the played card, the originally attempted illegal card is not lost and returns to hand.
- This immediate correction does not grant a correction reward.

Rebellion correction:
- On your turn, you may declare and fix one or more already existing board errors.
- The fixer chooses removal order and which offending card(s) to remove.
- If one correction reveals another error, the same fixer may continue correcting in the same turn.

Resolution effects:
- Removed offending cards lose attached protection/buildings.
- The fixer gains 1 Protection token for each corrected error (multiple corrections in the same turn grant multiple tokens).
- If one card removal resolves multiple errors at once, gain 1 Protection token for each error resolved.
- Each gained Protection token must be placed immediately on a legal eligible face-up map card.
- If no legal map placement exists, the fixer may park that token on their Daimyo reserve (max 3).
- If no legal map placement exists and the Daimyo reserve is full, that token is lost and returned to supply.

Treason/coordination:
- Intentional error play can be used strategically.
- Allied players may coordinate around error timing.
- However, all players can still call an error immediately before turn handoff.

RULE:
- There is no extra direct penalty for intentional illegal placement beyond correction removal and reward transfer.

TIP:
- Error fixing is both defensive cleanup and offensive removal timing.

## 9) CARDS AND TOKENS
Token semantics are strict: Koku is currency, while tokens placed on the board are Protection tokens.

Rules:
- A token placed on a board card protects that card.
- Protected cards cannot be removed directly unless an effect removes protection first or explicitly bypasses protection.
- In stacks, protection applies to the top visible layer.
- Stacking is allowed on Numbered cards, Retainer cards, face-down Numbered placeholders, and face-down Shinobi/Ronin traps.
- Protection tokens cannot be placed on face-down cards by action, effect, or correction.
- A Capital must be placed over a face-down base card and becomes the protection for that stack.
- Protection tokens can be placed only on Numbered cards or Retainer cards that are not already protected (maximum one piece per card: 1 Protection token, or 1 Market, or 1 Castle).
- Daimyo can hold up to 3 parked reserve Protection tokens; these tokens are not Koku and do not make Daimyo "protected."
- Tokens gained from error correction may be parked on Daimyo reserve only when immediate legal map placement is impossible; if reserve is full, excess is discarded.

### Daimyo Card
- Daimyo is placed on the Game Board during Junbi and then remains on the map.
- can hold up to 3 reserved build tokens (Protection tokens only) in its reserve slots; these do not provide automatic protection.
- Reserved tokens can be removed by card effects (for example steal/kill effects) if targeted legally.
- Koku tokens are never placed on the Daimyo card.

Gameplay terms used in this chapter:
- Adjacent: horizontal, vertical, or diagonal contact.
- Connected: horizontal or vertical contact only.
- Chain of Command: connected group that includes your Daimyo.

## 10) TOKENS TYPES

[HISTORICAL NOTE] *Koku* (石) was a real unit tied to rice yield (about 180 liters). It was widely used to measure wealth and a domain’s productivity.
A lord was typically considered daimyō-class at 10,000 koku or more.

The current system separates currency and map protection.

### Koku
- Koku is the main currency and is represented by physical Koku tokens.
- Koku tokens are kept on the player board area (not on map cards).
- Koku is spent to pay specific actions (for example Reveal) and can be converted into map protection.
- Counting cap and storage cap are separate:
  - End-of-turn economy counting can generate at most 5 Koku from counting sources.
  - Stored Koku cap is 6. Any Koku above 6 is discarded.

### Protection Tokens
- Protection tokens are placed on map cards.
- They provide defense and are also used as building progress for Castles and Market.
- Koku is the main currency. Protection tokens are generated by converting Koku during your turn.
- Turn Procedure — Convert Koku: You may convert any amount of Koku into Protection tokens at a rate of 2 Koku -> 1 Protection token.

### Daimyo Reserve (Protection-only reserve on the Daimyo card)
- The Daimyo card may hold up to 3 reserve Protection tokens.
- Tokens in the Daimyo reserve are still Protection tokens (not Koku).
- Daimyo reserve tokens do not protect the Daimyo automatically.
- Daimyo reserve tokens may be committed to building conversion when allowed by the Building rules.

### Player Supply (Off-map Components)
- Each player keeps a personal supply pile next to their board (Protection tokens, Koku, Castles, and other components) that are not currently on the map.
- Koku tokens should normally remain in the Player Board area; they are never placed on map cards or on the Daimyo card.
- When an effect steals Protection tokens and the target has fewer tokens on the map than required, the remaining tokens are taken from that player's personal supply.

### Alliance Tokens (Expert Mode)
- Alliance tokens mark active alliances and are placed on Daimyo cards.
- An Alliance token is a Protection token of the allied player's color.
- Alliance tokens are not spendable as normal Protection tokens.
- If an Alliance token is removed, the alliance ends immediately.

RULE:
- Breaking/removing an Alliance token does not grant bonus tokens to the attacker unless a card explicitly says so.

ILLUSTRATION: token-type overview with board examples and icon callouts.

### Quick Reference: Spend Koku vs Spend Protection

**Spend Koku (currency):**
- **Reveal (Bribery): 3 Koku** to reveal any face-down card (including your own).
- **Convert to Protection (Turn Procedure):** 2 Koku → 1 Protection token (you may convert any amount you currently have).

**Spend/Use Protection tokens (map resources):**
- **Place for defense:** place 1 Protection token on an eligible face-up card (max 1 piece per card: 1 token *or* 1 building).
- **Building progression:** commit/remove **3 Protection tokens** to create **1 Castle or 1 Market** (per Building rules).
- Protection tokens are **not** currency and are **never** converted back into Koku.

## 11) DEFENCES
Defense is built through layered control: Protection tokens, Castles, Capital positioning, and interrupt Retainer effects.

Defensive layers:
- Protection token: first defensive layer on a card.
- Castle: strong defensive structure and progression step toward Capital.
- Capital: highest structural defense and Shogun trigger requirement.
- Interrupt Retainers (for example Samurai-like effects): reactive defense against key actions.

RULE:
- In stacked cards, building-based protection applies to the current top card.
- If an error removes the top layer containing a building, the building is removed with that layer.

TIP:
- Build defensive depth by combining Protection tokens with chain connectivity, not by relying on a single structure.

NOTE: 
- Imperial Zones cannot be protected with Protection tokens.

## 12) BUILDING CASTLES AND MARKET

[HISTORICAL NOTE] During the Sengoku era, castles became administrative and military hubs. Castle towns (*jōkamachi*) grew around them as concentrated centers of power, trade, and logistics.

Buildings are created through Protection-token progression and are not purchased directly with Koku.

Base requirement:
- 3 Protection tokens available on connected cards in your Chain of Command.
- At least one valid connected Numbered card available as destination.

Process:
- Remove the 3 committed tokens.
- Place 1 Castle or 1 Market on an eligible **Numbered** card in your Chain of Command with **no existing building** (Castle/Market/Capital) on that card.
- The destination can be any eligible connected Numbered card; it does not need to be one of the cards that provided the tokens.

Mandatory transformation rule:
- Automatic conversion is mandatory only if you have at least 3 Protection tokens on connected **Numbered** cards and a valid connected Numbered destination exists.
- Remove any 3 eligible committed tokens from your connected chain (common case: 3 tokens on 3 Numbered cards).
- Committed tokens may come from Numbered cards, Retainer cards, and Daimyo parked reserve tokens, but the building destination must always be a valid connected Numbered card.
- If the 3 committed tokens include one or more tokens from Retainer cards and/or Daimyo reserve, the conversion is legal but not mandatory.

Stack clarification:
- A Castle protects the top layer of a stack.
- If an error removes that top layer, the Castle is removed with it; lower layers remain and are checked normally.

Market clarification:
- Market follows the same placement constraints as Castle.
- Defensively, a Market behaves like one protection layer and can be removed by legal Retainer effects or by error correction.

NOTE:
- Max 3 Castles per player.
- Max 1 Market per player.
- If a Castle conversion would create another 4th Castle, you may relocate one of your existing Castles to a legal connected Numbered destination.
- If no legal relocation is possible, the Castle conversion cannot resolve and the tokens remain in place.
- If 3 connected Castles are present at any point, Capital conversion remains mandatory (Section 13).

NOTE: 
- Imperial Zones cannot be used to built any Castle or Building.

TIP:
- Use Market to stabilize income and Castles to stabilize board presence.

## 13) FORMING A CAPITAL
Capital formation is the final structural progression step.
Castles are progressed through Protection tokens and are not purchased directly with Koku.

Requirements:
- 3 Castles in your Chain of Command.
- Continuous connectivity to your Daimyo.

Process:
- Capital Placement is mandatory: when you complete the third connected Castle, remove the 3 Castles and place 1 Capital.
- The Capital is always placed on a face-down base card.
- If the chosen base card is a connected face-up Numbered card, turn it face-down first, then place the Capital.
- If there is already a face-down card at the chosen connected Capital position (including a hidden trap), use it directly as the Capital base.
- While the Capital remains on top, that base card is locked and cannot be attacked, revealed, or destroyed by effects.

Connectivity rule:
- If one of the required Castles becomes disconnected, Capital conversion cannot occur until chain connectivity is restored.

Castle-count handling:
- In disconnected situations, a player may rebuild a connected Castle line before conversion.
- The player cannot delay conversion once 3 connected Castles are valid.

RULE: 
- The current ruleset uses a single active Capital path (max 1 active Capital state per player for Shogun progression).

TIP:
- Prioritize resilient chain routes before converting to Capital, to reduce declaration risk.

ILLUSTRATION: castle-to-capital conversion sequence with before/after map snippets.
RULE:
- Disconnected Castles do not contribute to Capital progression.
- To recover progression, the player may reconnect the chain and/or create a new connected Castle by relocating one existing Castle (see Section 12 Castle-limit handling).

## 14) ATTACK ACTIONS
Attacks are resolved through Retainer actions, error correction, and trap/reveal effects.

Attack scope:
- Valid effects can target cards, protection, and structures according to card text.
- Retainers can affect Numbered cards and, where specified, other card types.

Resolution order:
- Resolve attack effects one at a time, in play order.
- Apply each effect fully before resolving the next effect.

Sacrifice (voluntary):
- On your turn, you may remove your own Castle or Capital and return the piece to supply.
- Use this only when repositioning or chain recovery is strategically necessary.

- There is no "declare all attacks then resolve" sub-step.

## 15) BECAME A SHOGUN

[HISTORICAL NOTE] The title *Seii Taishōgun* was formally bestowed by the Emperor, providing imperial legitimacy to a military ruler (even when real power was contested).
Example: in 1603 Tokugawa Ieyasu received the shogun title from Emperor Go-Yōzei, often treated as the start of the Tokugawa shogunate.
Related episode: Akechi Mitsuhide is often remembered as the self-proclaimed "thirteen-day shogun" for his short-lived bid to seize power after Honnō-ji.

A player who completes a connected Capital becomes a Shogun Candidate. Shogun is confirmed later during endgame checks.

Shogun Candidacy Declaration (during your turn):
1) Complete a Capital that is connected to your Chain of Command.
2) Immediately declare your candidacy as Shogun (mandatory; this declaration cannot be skipped).
3) Start the Final Cycle: all other players take one final turn in turn order.
4) During the Final Cycle, if your Capital becomes disconnected from your Chain of Command, your candidacy becomes invalid unless you reconnect before candidate confirmation.

Endgame sequence:
- This round is called the Shogunate Final Cycle.
- When the Shogunate Final Cycle starts, all active alliances are immediately broken and alliance tokens are returned to the discarded token pool.
- During the Final Cycle, other players may also satisfy the Capital condition and declare Shogun candidacy.

Candidate confirmation step (Koku-only):
- After all final turns are completed, resolve a Candidate Confirmation step in turn order (starting from the anchor player).
- Only players who currently have a valid connected Capital may act in this step.
- In this step, valid candidates may spend Koku on legal Koku-based effects only.
- Players without a valid connected Capital do not act in this step.
- When a candidate's confirmation timing is reached and their Capital is still valid and connected, that player is confirmed as Shogun.

Final action window:
- Shogun Candidates may still use legal defensive/interruption Retainers during the Shogunate Final Cycle when a card/rule allows it.
- Koku can still be spent for legal reveal/defensive decisions during the Shogunate Final Cycle.

NOTE:
- Shogun confirmation is condition-based and depends on Capital connectivity at candidate confirmation timing.

## 16) ALLIANCES
Alliances are part of Expert Mode (together with Visit the Emperor and Destiny Path objectives).

[HISTORICAL NOTE] Sengoku politics relied heavily on shifting coalitions. Alliances were often pragmatic and temporary, and could be reinforced through diplomacy (e.g., negotiated pledges and marriage ties). Many alliances ended in betrayal, including Akechi Mitsuhide's famous defection and multiple Daimyo reversals at Sekigahara.

Formation:
- Alliances are formed according to the Expert-mode conditions printed on player boards.
- When formed, alliance markers/tokens are placed on Daimyo cards as a pact indicator.

RULE:
- The alliance marker is a Protection token of the allied player's color placed on the Daimyo.
- While used as an alliance marker, it cannot be spent or moved as a normal Protection token unless an effect explicitly removes it.

Effects:
- Alliance effects and constraints are applied as defined by current Expert-mode board text.
- Alliance state is public information.

Breaking alliances:
- If an Alliance token is removed by effect, the alliance ends immediately.
- Breaking an alliance does not automatically award bonus resources unless an effect explicitly says so.

RULE:
- Alliance handling belongs to Expert Mode only; Standard Mode ignores this chapter.

NOTE:
- If this chapter conflicts with older text, Expert-mode player-board text has priority.
RULE:
- In 5-6 player Expert Mode (9x9 board), each player may maintain at most 2 simultaneous alliances.

## 17) VISIT THE EMPEROR

[HISTORICAL NOTE] The Imperial Court in Kyoto remained a symbolic source of legitimacy. Warlords often sought court titles or imperial recognition to strengthen their claims.
Example: Toyotomi Hideyoshi pursued court legitimacy and became *kampaku* (Imperial Regent) in 1585.

Visit the Emperor is an Expert Mode rule and is played together with Alliances and Destiny Path.

Core effect:
- The action enables a deck reset/reshuffle flow through each player's Emperor Visit right.
- The reshuffle can be used once per match.
- Emperor Visit marker state must be visible (available vs used/lost), for example by flipping/turning the marker.

Deck-exhaustion handling:
- At first deck exhaustion during a required draw (start/end turn or effect), if the Emperor Visit right is unused, perform a normal deck reshuffle (discard -> new deck).
- In this automatic exhaustion case, the Emperor Visit right is lost immediately and the marker is flipped/turned to used-lost state.
- If this happens during beginning refill, resolve this automatic procedure first; afterward, no proactive Visit the Emperor can be declared in that same turn.
- If deck exhaustion happens again, no second reshuffle is available.
- After the one allowed reshuffle, players keep drawing in normal order from whatever deck remains; if no cards are available, they do not draw.

RULE:
- Emperor-visit usage and marker state must be visible to all players.

NOTE:
- Treat this as Expert-only timing control and resource-management pressure, not as a base-mode action.
RULE:
- Visit the Emperor can be declared proactively only at the beginning of your own turn, after the beginning refill and before playing your first card, as long as your Emperor marker has not already been used.
- In proactive Visit the Emperor, the player reshuffles deck+discard and may optionally include the entire hand (all cards in hand, or none).
- If the player includes the entire hand, they draw back up to 3 cards immediately after the reshuffle.
- If the player does not include hand cards, no additional draw is performed by this Visit action.
- After proactive use, flip/turn the Emperor marker to used state; it cannot be used again.
- Outside this timing window (mid-turn, end-turn, or while resolving effects), proactive Visit the Emperor is not allowed.

## 18) TURN STRUCTURE
A turn has three sections:

1) Beginning of turn
- Draw/refill back up to **3** cards. If you already have 3 cards, you draw 0.
- This beginning refill exists to recover from off-turn effects that may have reduced your hand before your turn starts.
- Expert Mode timing: if Emperor marker is unused, you may declare Visit the Emperor now (after refill, before your first played card).

2) Main turn section
- Play cards down to 2 cards in hand.
- You may play one additional last card by paying **1 Koku** (ending with 1 card in hand).
- Turn Procedure — Convert Koku (2 Koku -> 1 Protection token, any amount during your turn), then place Protection tokens.
- Build Market/Castle/Capital when conditions are met.
- Reveal (Bribery): Pay 3 Koku to reveal any face-down card (including your own).
- Optionally: fix errors, make/break alliances (Expert Mode), and declare Shogun candidacy when valid.

3) End of turn
- Draw/refill back up to **3** cards for your next turn.
- Collect Koku/resources.
- Pass turn clockwise.

RULE:
- Full error-fix actions and action execution happen only during the main turn section.
- Retainer Order Actions and Turn Procedures are separate systems.
- Reveal, Convert Koku, Build, and Error Fix may all be performed in the same turn if legal (there is no global action-point limit).
- Off-turn players may still call an immediate illegal placement before turn handoff, and the acting player corrects it immediately (no reward).
- During end of turn, no new actions are executed and Koku is only collected/applied.
- The Player Board repeats economy values as a quick reference; use this rulebook for timing, caps, and edge cases.
- Economy summary (see Player Board for values): End of turn, gain Koku from Base income, Market, Controlled Zones or Lines, and Controlled Imperial Zones.
- Income from Zones and Imperial Zones stacks.
- Example: if you control all 4 Imperial Zones and also control 1 Zone, you gain +5 Koku from these two sources (4 + 1).
- End-of-turn economy counting generates at most 5 Koku from counting sources.
- Income is collected from all valid sources; players do not selectively skip sources to avoid cap waste.
- Any Koku gained above the 6 Koku cap is discarded.
- If deck runs out, follow the active mode rule:
- Standard Mode: reshuffle discard as new draw deck.
- Expert Mode with Visit the Emperor: at first required-draw exhaustion, do the same normal reshuffle and mark Emperor Visit as lost if it was unused.
- In normal flow, the end-of-turn refill already restores your hand to 3; the beginning refill often draws 0 and mainly exists to recover from off-turn hand loss.

RULE:
- Off-turn reactions are allowed only when a card/rule explicitly opens a reaction window.

## 19) END GAME SEQUENCE (STANDARD)
Endgame starts when a player declares Shogun candidacy with a valid connected Capital condition.

Shogun trigger:
- 1 connected Capital is sufficient.

Final-round anchor:
- The first player who declares Shogun candidacy is the anchor player.
- After declaration, all other players take one final turn in turn order.

Multiple Shogun declarations:
- Other players may also satisfy the Capital condition and declare Shogun candidacy during the Shogunate Final Cycle.
- All candidates who still have a valid connected Capital at confirmation timing are considered in scoring.

Final-phase interaction:
- Samurai-style interruptions are allowed in the Shogunate Final Cycle only as legal defensive reactions.
- If chain/capital validity is broken before candidate confirmation, the affected player's candidacy is invalid until reconnected.

Candidate confirmation / closure:
- After the table-wide Final Cycle ends, resolve the Candidate Confirmation step (Koku-only) in turn order starting from the anchor player.
- Only players with a currently valid connected Capital act in this step and can spend Koku on legal Koku-based effects.
- When the Candidate Confirmation step ends, scoring begins immediately.

RULE:
- Declaring Shogun candidacy is mandatory when you complete a connected Capital.
- A player may lose and later recover candidacy by losing/reconnecting Capital chain validity before candidate confirmation.

## 20) FINAL SCORING (STANDARD)
Scoring uses connected control relative to each player's Chain of Command.

Score:
- +1 each face-up Numbered or Retainer card on board.
- +1 each full zone or line under control.
- +1 each Castle.
- +3 each Capital.
- +4 if player is a confirmed Shogun.
- +1 if (Expert Mode) the player never used their Emperor reshuffle right.
- Losing Emperor right by automatic deck-exhaustion handling counts as "used" (no +1 bonus).

Control definition:
- "Under your control" means the relevant zone/line is fully occupied by your clan cards (face-down cards still count as your clan).
- In stacked spaces, only the top card determines control; a face-down top card still counts as its clan.
- Chain of Command adjacency is edge-to-edge only (no diagonal chain links).

RULE:
- Face-down cards do not score the direct +1 card value.
- Face-down cards still count for zone/line control when they belong to your clan.

Connectivity rule:
- Only connected cards/structures score.
- Disconnected cards, Castles, and Capitals do not score until reconnected.

Alliance token scoring:
- Alliance tokens do not add direct score by default.
- They matter only through alliance effects unless a specific card/effect states otherwise.


NOTES/TIPS:
- Becoming a Shogun Candidate does not guarantee victory.
- Large connected protected areas usually score better than scattered expansion.

## 21) LET'S START! PREPARATION PHASE - JUNBI
Junbi is the initial placement phase used to establish starting Daimyo positions.

[HISTORICAL NOTE] *Junbi* (準備) means "preparation." In Sengoku warfare, pre-battle preparation covered deployment order, supply readiness, and command-line positioning before engagement.

Preparation steps:
1. Determine first player.
2. First player places Daimyo on a legal starting edge location.
3. Next players place Daimyo clockwise, each respecting Daimyo Sudoku restrictions (no same row, column, or zone conflict with already placed Daimyo cards).
4. Once all Daimyo cards are placed, normal turn flow begins.

Setup clarification:
- During setup, each Daimyo card is parked beside/on the Player Board until its Junbi placement turn.
- This preparation phase uses Daimyo placement only.
- Numbered-card setup placement is not part of this phase in the current ruleset.

Starting locations:
- Starting Daimyo locations are specific edge positions marked for setup.
- Coordinate reference (6x6): B1, D1, C1, E1, A2, F2, A3, F3, A4, F4, A5, F5, B6, C6, D6, E6.
- Coordinates for 5x5 (Duels) and 9x9 setups are different and should be documented in their dedicated setup diagrams.
- Only the listed starting locations are valid; corner prefectures are not valid starting positions.

Fallback rule:
- If a player must redraw for setup-hand constraints, reshuffle and redraw until at least one valid Numbered card is available for normal play.

## 23) RULE CHANGES (DUELS)
Duels mode uses tighter connectivity and board-pressure rules.

Chain of Command:
- Cards must remain connected to the Daimyo chain to stay fully active.
- Disconnected cards lose scoring and tactical value until reconnected.
- Disconnected buildings do not provide income/scoring while disconnected.

Bridge (center space) rule:
- The center bridge zone grants +1 income when controlled and connected.
- Cards may be placed there if legal.
- Buildings cannot be placed on the bridge space.

Economy and actions:
- Core Koku/protection rules remain active.
- Expert-only modules (Alliances / Visit the Emperor) are optional and should be enabled only if both players agree before setup.

RULE:
- Duels follows the same attack-resolution order defined in Section 14.
- Disconnected cards lose chain-based effects immediately at disconnection.

## 24) END GAME (DUELS)

- If the opponent also satisfies the Capital condition in that final turn, both players may become Shogun Candidates.
- After that final turn, resolve the Candidate Confirmation step (Koku-only): only players with a valid connected Capital may act, in order, spending only Koku on legal Koku-based effects.
- After the Candidate Confirmation step, the game ends and scoring begins.
